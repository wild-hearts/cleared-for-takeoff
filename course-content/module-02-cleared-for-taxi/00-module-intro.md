# Module 2: Cleared For Taxi
## Module Introduction Script

**Spoken duration:** ~2.5 minutes  
**Format:** Direct to camera, no slides  
**Tone:** Warm, slightly more energetic than Module 1 — we're past the setup, into the skill  

---

[LOOK TO CAMERA]

Let me address the thing that stops most people before they've properly started.

[PAUSE]

"I'm going to ask it the wrong thing and look stupid."

[PAUSE]

I hear this constantly. And I understand it. We have spent decades being evaluated on how we phrase questions. In school, in job interviews, in professional settings — the way you ask something signals how much you know. Asking the wrong question can be embarrassing.

[PAUSE]

So let me say this clearly, and then we'll move on.

The AI doesn't care. It has no opinion of you. It is not judging the quality of your question. It is not filing away that you asked something obvious. It will not think less of you if your first attempt is messy, or vague, or completely misses what you were trying to say.

You can ask it ten bad questions in a row and it will answer all ten with the same patience. There is no score being kept.

[PAUSE]

Module 2 is about learning to talk to AI in a way that gets you useful results. Not perfect results — useful ones. And the gap between a prompt that gives you something frustrating and a prompt that gives you something genuinely helpful is smaller than you think.

[PAUSE]

Six lessons. About 75 minutes total.

Lesson one: why typing at AI like it's a search engine doesn't work, and what to do instead.

Lesson two: the four things every good prompt includes. You don't have to memorise them.

Lesson three: how to ask AI to interview you before it helps you — and when that's more useful than just jumping in.

Lesson four: when to be specific, when to leave things open, and when to start the whole conversation over.

Lesson five: the mistakes most people make in their first week, and how to avoid them.

Lesson six: how to save and reuse the prompts that work, so you're not reinventing this every time.

[PAUSE]

Let's go.
