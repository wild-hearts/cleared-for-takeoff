# Module 6, Lesson 4: Where to Go Next — Paid Tools and What Is Worth It

**Spoken duration:** ~10 minutes  
**Word count (spoken):** ~1,320 words  
**Format:** Direct to camera; on-screen comparison  
**On-screen text:** Free vs. paid comparison; decision guide  
**Tone:** Practical, direct, honest — no upselling, no inflated claims  

---

[LOOK TO CAMERA]

Every major AI tool has a free tier and a paid tier. The free tier is where most people start, and it is perfectly capable. The paid tier costs money — typically between twenty and thirty Australian dollars a month — and offers more.

[PAUSE]

This lesson is about when the paid version is worth it, and when it is not. I will try to be as honest as I can about this, because the answer is genuinely: it depends on you.

[PAUSE]

[ON SCREEN: "What paid tiers generally offer"]

Paid subscriptions to the major AI tools typically offer some combination of:

More powerful models — newer, more capable versions of the AI that perform better on complex tasks.
Higher usage limits — the free tier has limits on how many messages you can send per day. Paid users have much higher limits.
More memory or context — the ability to work with longer documents or maintain more context across a longer conversation.
Priority access — faster response times and access to new features first.
Additional tools — image generation, file analysis, web search, and other capabilities that are restricted on free tiers.

[PAUSE]

[ON SCREEN: "When free is enough"]

The free tier is enough if:

You use AI a few times a week for everyday tasks — drafting, summarising, explaining, planning.
You are not hitting message limits with your current use.
The tasks you use AI for do not require the most powerful available model.

[PAUSE]

Most of what we have covered in this course can be done on a free tier. Drafting letters. Summarising documents. Brainstorming. Research questions. Health information. Planning trips and events.

If you have been using the free tier throughout this course and it has met your needs, there is no urgency to change that.

[PAUSE]

[ON SCREEN: "When paid is worth considering"]

The paid tier is worth considering if:

You are hitting usage limits regularly — you are trying to use AI and being told you have reached your daily limit.
You need to work with long documents — the full text of a contract, a submission, a research report — and the free tier cannot hold the whole thing in one conversation.
You are using AI for something significant — a business task, a writing project, ongoing professional use — where the improvement in quality from a more powerful model justifiably has value.
You want image generation — creating visual content using AI requires the paid tier on most platforms.
You want to use the web search feature consistently — some platforms include this only in paid tiers.

[PAUSE]

If you are using AI daily for tasks that matter to you, and you regularly find yourself limited by the free tier, the cost of a paid subscription is likely to be worth it — particularly compared to the time value of what AI is saving you.

[PAUSE]

[ON SCREEN: "The tools and their paid tiers — current as of 2025"]

[NOTE TO PRODUCTION: This slide should be reviewed at production time for current pricing — AI subscription costs change. The following are approximate as of early 2025.]

ChatGPT Plus: approximately $28 AUD per month. Gives access to GPT-4o and newer models, higher limits, image generation, web search, and file analysis.

Claude Pro: approximately $30 AUD per month. Gives access to Claude's more capable models, higher usage limits, and priority access to new features.

Google Gemini Advanced: approximately $35 AUD per month as part of Google One AI Premium. Gives access to Gemini's most capable model, with integration into Google Workspace.

Microsoft Copilot Pro: approximately $38 AUD per month. Integrates AI more deeply into Word, Excel, Outlook, and other Microsoft 365 applications.

[BACK TO CAMERA]

[PAUSE]

None of these prices are fixed permanently. Check the current pricing on each platform's website before you subscribe.

[PAUSE]

[ON SCREEN: "A practical decision guide"]

Here is a simple decision guide.

[PAUSE]

If you are a light user — a few times a week, straightforward tasks — stay on free until you hit a limitation that bothers you.

If you are a regular user — most days, meaningful tasks — trial a paid tier for one month and see whether the improvement in capability and the removal of limits is worth the cost. Most platforms offer a one-month cancellation policy.

If you are a Copilot user and you already pay for Microsoft 365 — check whether Copilot is already included in your subscription level. For some plans, it is.

If you are a heavy Google Workspace user — Gmail, Docs, Sheets, Drive — the Gemini Advanced integration into those tools may have particular value for your workflow.

[PAUSE]

[ON SCREEN: "A note on value"]

The question is not "is AI worth paying for?" It is "is this specific subscription worth what it costs for how I actually use it?"

[PAUSE]

If you save three hours a month — which is a conservative estimate for regular AI users on meaningful tasks — and your time has any value at all, the cost of a subscription is very likely covered.

[PAUSE]

If you are not using it enough for three hours of time saving to be plausible, stay on free and build your use first. Then reconsider.

[PAUSE]

There is no answer here that is right for everyone. But there is an answer that is right for your level of use and your situation. You now have the framework to find it.

[PAUSE]

One more practical note: you do not have to subscribe to the same tool forever. If you start with ChatGPT and find Claude suits you better for a particular use, it is straightforward to switch. Most people find they have a preference after a few months of regular use, and the difference between platforms is smaller than the marketing suggests. Find the one that suits how you work and stay with it.
