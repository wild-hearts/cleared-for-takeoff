# Module 6, Intro: Final Approach

**Spoken duration:** ~3 minutes  
**Word count (spoken):** ~420 words  
**Format:** Direct to camera  
**On-screen text:** Module title; lesson list  

---

[LOOK TO CAMERA]

There is a fear that surfaces at this stage, and it is a reasonable one.

[PAUSE]

"AI keeps changing. It changes fast. Things that are true now might not be true in six months. How do I stay current? How do I make sure what I have learned does not go stale?"

[PAUSE]

This module addresses that directly.

[PAUSE]

The answer is: the skills you have developed in this course are not AI-specific. They are thinking skills applied to AI. How to ask a clear question. How to evaluate a response. How to know when to trust and when to verify. How to use a tool well rather than just using it.

[PAUSE]

Tools change. Thinking skills do not.

[PAUSE]

The specific platforms will update, add features, change their pricing, and occasionally change their name. New ones will appear. Old ones will be superseded. None of that makes what you have learned obsolete. It means you will apply these skills to a slightly different interface, the same way you applied your ability to write a letter to email, and will apply it again to whatever comes next.

[PAUSE]

[ON SCREEN: Module 6 lesson list]

Six lessons in this module:

Lesson 1: Building your routine — one page, one habit, one tool.
Lesson 2: When not to use AI — the strongest sign of understanding.
Lesson 3: Staying current — following AI without panic.
Lesson 4: Where to go next — paid tools and when they are worth it.
Lesson 5: Bringing someone along — how to share this with a partner, friend, or family member.
Lesson 6: Final reflection and graduation.

[BACK TO CAMERA]

[PAUSE]

This is the last module. But it is not an ending. It is a transition to what comes after a course — the part where you stop studying something and start doing it.

[PAUSE]

Let's finish well.
