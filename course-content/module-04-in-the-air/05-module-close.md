# Module 4, Close: In The Air — Module Close

**Spoken duration:** ~4 minutes  
**Word count (spoken):** ~530 words  
**Format:** Direct to camera  
**On-screen text:** Module 4 Cleared To Try; transition to Module 5  

---

[LOOK TO CAMERA]

You have just spent time with AI on something that matters to you. Not a demo. Not an exercise. Something real.

[PAUSE]

That is the point where most courses stop — with a demonstration of what is possible. This course does not stop there. It asks you to actually do it.

[PAUSE]

You may have found the track session gave you more than you expected. That the conversation went somewhere you did not anticipate. That AI asked a question that unlocked something you had not thought of.

[PAUSE]

Or you may have found it harder than expected. That you needed to try different approaches. That the first prompt did not work and you had to start over. That what you wanted was clearer in your head than it was on the page.

[PAUSE]

Both of those are fine. Both of those are learning.

[PAUSE]

[ON SCREEN: "Module 4 — Cleared To Try"]

Your Cleared To Try for this module: commit two hours to your chosen track before the end of this week.

Not two hours of reading about it. Two hours of working on it. Writing, planning, learning, building — whichever track you chose.

If you find yourself spending the whole two hours on one part of the problem — a single section, a single lesson, a single planning question — that is fine. Depth is more useful than breadth in this stage.

At the end of the two hours: write one sentence about what you made or learned or clarified. Just one sentence. It does not have to be impressive. It just has to be true.

[BACK TO CAMERA]

[PAUSE]

Come back to the other tracks when the time comes. The writing track will be there when the writing is ready. The starting track will be there when the idea is ready. The sorting track will be there when the pile becomes unavoidable.

[PAUSE]

Module 5 is different in character from everything we have done so far.

[PAUSE]

Module 5 is called Holding Pattern, and it addresses the things that deserve honest attention: what AI gets wrong and how to spot it. How scams are using AI and what to watch for. What you should never put into an AI tool. How to build the habit of trusting and verifying at the same time.

[PAUSE]

These are not reasons to be afraid of AI. They are reasons to be informed.

[PAUSE]

Confident AI users are not people who never question what they are told. They are people who know when to ask a second question, where to verify, and what belongs in the chat versus what belongs in a private conversation with a professional.

[PAUSE]

See you there.
