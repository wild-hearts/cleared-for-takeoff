# Module 4, Intro: In The Air

**Spoken duration:** ~4 minutes  
**Word count (spoken):** ~540 words  
**Format:** Direct to camera  
**On-screen text:** Module title; track menu  

---

[LOOK TO CAMERA]

There is a question I hear underneath a lot of conversations about AI.

[PAUSE]

Not "how does it work" and not "is it safe." The quieter question: "Will I actually use it? Once the novelty wears off — will it really have a place in my daily life?"

[PAUSE]

That is a fair question. And the honest answer is: it depends on what you point it at.

[PAUSE]

People who use AI consistently are not people with more technical knowledge. They are people who found something that mattered to them — something they wanted to write, start, sort, or learn — and discovered that AI could help them make progress on it.

[PAUSE]

That is what Module 4 is designed to do.

[PAUSE]

[ON SCREEN: Module 4 track menu]

This module has four tracks. Each one is a longer session — designed for real work on a real project.

You pick one track. You spend this module actually doing it.

The tracks are:

Track A: Write something. A memoir, a family history, a eulogy, a speech, a collection of stories, a piece of writing that has been waiting for the right tool.

Track B: Start something. A business idea, a community project, a side income, a Substack, something you have been turning over in your mind.

Track C: Sort something. A renovation, a retirement plan, a trip you keep putting off, an estate, a life transition that needs a proper plan.

Track D: Learn something. A language, a skill, a subject, a creative pursuit — with AI as your tutor and practice partner.

[BACK TO CAMERA]

[PAUSE]

One track. This module.

[PAUSE]

The sessions in this module are longer than what you have done so far — they go deeper into a single project rather than covering multiple examples. That is intentional. The difference between knowing AI can help and actually making progress on something significant is the difference between a demonstration and doing it.

[PAUSE]

Choose the track that reflects something you genuinely want. Not the track that seems most practical or impressive. The one that, if it worked out, would feel like something.

[PAUSE]

You can come back to the other tracks. There is no expiry. But today, pick one and go.

---
*End of Module 4 Intro. Run time approximately 4 minutes.*
