# Module 1, Lesson 6: Your Dashboard and Your First Hello

**Spoken duration:** ~9 minutes  
**Word count (spoken):** ~1,150 words  
**Format:** Direct to camera with brief cutaway to show chat interface  
**On-screen text:** The exact first prompt, displayed clearly  
**Note to production:** This lesson ends with the Cleared To Try exercise. Deliver it warmly — this is the moment the student does the thing for the first time.  

---

[LOOK TO CAMERA]

You have an account. It's set up. Now let's take a look at what you're actually dealing with.

[PAUSE]

The interface of an AI tool is, honestly, one of the least intimidating things about it.

[PAUSE]

It is, at its core, a chat window. A text box at the bottom of the screen. A conversation history above it. And a button to send what you've typed.

That's it. If you've ever sent a text message, or used Facebook Messenger, or written an email — you already know how to navigate this interface. The learning is not in the buttons. The learning is in what you type.

[PAUSE]

Let me give you a quick tour of the elements you'll see.

[ON SCREEN: annotated screenshot or screen recording of a chat interface, elements highlighted as named]

The text box. This is where you type. You can type one sentence or ten paragraphs. You hit enter or click the send button.

The conversation area. Everything you and the AI say to each other appears here. Your messages on one side, the AI's on the other. As the conversation gets longer, you scroll up to see earlier parts of it.

The sidebar or history panel. On most platforms, your past conversations are listed here. You can click on any of them to go back and continue.

The settings icon. Usually in the top right or bottom left of the screen. This is where you found the settings we configured in the last lesson.

The new conversation button. Usually looks like a pen icon or a "+" symbol. Clicking it starts a fresh conversation — a blank slate, with no memory of previous ones.

[BACK TO CAMERA]

[PAUSE]

That is the whole interface. You now know as much about it as you need to.

[PAUSE]

One thing I want to flag, because it trips people up:

When you see the AI's response appearing word by word — like it's being typed in real time — that's normal. The AI generates its response progressively rather than all at once. Wait for it to finish before you respond. There's usually a stop button if you want it to stop mid-response.

[PAUSE]

Good. Now we're going to do the thing.

[PAUSE]

[ON SCREEN: The following text displayed clearly]

Your first prompt. Type this, exactly:

"Hello. I'm completely new to AI and I'd like to know three things about you. First: what can you do? Second: what can't you do? Third: what should I never ask you?"

[BACK TO CAMERA]

[PAUSE]

Why this prompt?

[PAUSE]

Because it does something specific. It asks the AI to tell you its own limits before you've relied on it for anything. It gets the "here's what I can't do" conversation out of the way at the start, on your terms, before it catches you out.

It also teaches you something immediately: AI is quite good at describing itself. The answers you get back will be clear, reasonable, and useful for calibrating your expectations going forward.

[PAUSE]

Type it. Hit send. Read what comes back.

[PAUSE]

[NOTE TO PRODUCTION: pause here for 10-15 seconds of silence while the viewer types. Hold on camera — warm, patient expression. Don't rush this moment.]

[PAUSE — hold for student]

[LOOK TO CAMERA]

[PAUSE]

If the AI gave you a confident, readable answer — you're off. That's all it takes. That's your first conversation.

[PAUSE]

Now try one more. Ask it something you're genuinely curious about. Not a test. Not a trick. Something you actually want to know.

How does X work. What's the best way to do Y. Explain Z to me in simple terms.

See what comes back.

[PAUSE]

[NOTE TO PRODUCTION: another brief pause — 8-10 seconds. Hold camera.]

[PAUSE]

That is the thing. That is the whole thing.

A text box. A question. An answer. A conversation.

[PAUSE]

[ON SCREEN: "Cleared To Try — Module 1"]

Before we close this module, here is your Cleared To Try exercise.

You've already started it. Now finish it.

Sign up to one tool — which you've done.
Say hello to it — which you've done.
Ask it three things about itself — which you've done.

One more step: ask it one question about something in your actual life. Not a practice question. A real one. Something that's been sitting in the back of your mind that you haven't gotten around to looking into.

A health question you've been meaning to research. A document you've been avoiding. A plan you haven't started. Give the AI one real task — however small — and see what happens.

[PAUSE]

You don't have to do anything with the answer right now. You're just noticing what it's like. How the conversation feels. What the AI gives you. What it doesn't give you.

Notice. That's the whole exercise.

[PAUSE]

[BACK TO CAMERA]

Module 1 is done.

[PAUSE]

You know what AI is. You know what it does well and what it doesn't. You have chosen a tool. You have a secure account with your privacy settings in order. You have had your first conversation.

[PAUSE]

That is not a small thing, if you came into this module not knowing any of it.

[PAUSE]

In Module 2, we get into the thing that makes the biggest difference to how useful AI actually is for you: how to talk to it. Because there's a way of asking things that produces good results, and a way that produces frustrating ones — and the difference is simpler than you think.

See you there.
