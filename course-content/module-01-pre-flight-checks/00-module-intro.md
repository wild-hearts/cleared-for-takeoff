# Module 1: Pre-Flight Checks
## Module Introduction Script

**Spoken duration:** ~2.5 minutes  
**Format:** Direct to camera, no slides  
**Tone:** Warm, unhurried, permission-giving  

---

[LOOK TO CAMERA]

Here's something I want to say before we start.

[PAUSE]

If you're sitting here having clicked on a course about AI and feeling quietly embarrassed that you don't already know how to use it — you're in excellent company.

Most people who seem confident with AI right now figured it out in private. They went through exactly what you're about to go through. They just did it in a different year, when nobody was watching. The gap between them and you is not ability. It's not intelligence. It's not even effort.

It's timing.

[PAUSE]

This module is called Pre-Flight Checks. And that's exactly what it is. The things you need to know before anything else. Not the most exciting content in the course. But the most important — because walking into this without them is how people get frustrated and give up.

Six lessons. Roughly sixty minutes total. Take as long as you need.

[PAUSE]

Lesson one: what AI actually is. Not the version with the jargon. The real version. The one that actually helps you use it.

Lesson two: what AI does well, and what it does badly. Both matter. Walking in with the wrong expectations is the fastest way to get this wrong.

Lesson three: how to choose your first tool. ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini, Copilot — what they are, how they differ, and why your choice matters a lot less than you think.

Lesson four: how to sign up safely. What to share, what to skip, and the one thing you should never type into a chat window.

Lesson five: how to set up your account properly. One setting in particular that most people miss entirely.

Lesson six: your first look around the interface, and your very first hello.

[PAUSE]

No speed required. No test at the end. Nobody is grading how long it takes.

The only goal at the end of this module is one tool, set up properly, and one first conversation completed.

That's it. Let's start.

---
*End of module intro. Cut to Lesson 1.1.*
