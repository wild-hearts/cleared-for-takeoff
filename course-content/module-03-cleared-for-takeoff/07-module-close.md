[LOOK TO CAMERA]

You have just done something most people never do.

[PAUSE]

Most people who start using AI pick one thing — email drafting, say, or looking things up — and stay there. They never test the edges. They never find out what else it can do.

[PAUSE]

You have now seen, in detail, how AI applies to six specific areas of real life. Not abstract examples. Not corporate scenarios. Real tasks: the letter you need to write, the document you need to understand, the trip you are trying to plan, the medication you want to know more about, the super statement sitting on the kitchen bench.

[PAUSE]

Some of the paths you chose will have felt immediately relevant. Others might have been useful to understand even if you did not need them today — because life changes, and what you need from AI changes with it.

[PAUSE]

Come back to the other paths when the time comes.

[PAUSE]

[ON SCREEN: "Module 3 — Cleared To Try"]

Your Cleared To Try exercise for this module.

Pick one real task from the past two weeks — something you dealt with, or did not deal with because it felt too hard — and complete it using AI.

Not a test task. Not a made-up scenario. A real thing.

The letter you should have sent. The document you kept putting aside. The plan you said you would make and did not. The information you looked up on Google for forty-five minutes without finding a clear answer.

Use the prompt structures from the paths you completed. Apply the four-part framework from Module 2.

Then notice two things:

How long did it take, compared to doing it without AI?
What would you have done differently in how you set up the prompt, knowing what you know now?

[BACK TO CAMERA]

[PAUSE]

Write those two things down. They are your data points. Over time, you will accumulate a lot of them — and they will tell you, concretely, where AI is earning its place in your life and where it is not.

[PAUSE]

Module 4 is where we go deeper.

[PAUSE]

In Module 4, you are going to choose one track — one significant project or goal — and use AI to actually work on it over the module. Not an exercise. Not a demonstration. A thing you actually want to do.

Write something. Start something. Sort something. Learn something.

[PAUSE]

These are longer sessions. They are designed for people who are ready to go beyond the basics and use AI for something that matters to them.

[PAUSE]

See you there.
