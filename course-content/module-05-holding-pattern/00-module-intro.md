# Module 5, Intro: Holding Pattern

**Spoken duration:** ~4 minutes  
**Word count (spoken):** ~520 words  
**Format:** Direct to camera  
**On-screen text:** Module title; lesson list  

---

[LOOK TO CAMERA]

You have been using AI. Hopefully you have had good experiences — things that worked, tasks that got easier, time that was saved.

[PAUSE]

Module 5 is the one where we talk honestly about the things that can go wrong.

[PAUSE]

This is not a module designed to frighten you. It is a module designed to make you a more confident, more capable, more discerning user.

[PAUSE]

The people who use AI most effectively are not the ones who trust it completely, and they are not the ones who distrust it reflexively. They are the ones who know the specific things to watch for, the questions to ask, and the situations where AI is not the right tool.

[PAUSE]

That is what this module covers.

[PAUSE]

There is a fear that surfaces at this stage for a lot of people. "I have been using AI more regularly, and now I am reading about the horror stories. What are the actual risks? Am I safe?"

[PAUSE]

The answer is: yes, with knowledge. AI use by an informed person is safe in the same way that driving is safe for someone who has learned properly, knows the rules, and pays attention. Not zero-risk — nothing is — but managed and reasonable.

[PAUSE]

The uninformed version is where the problems are. And this module is about making sure you are informed.

[PAUSE]

[ON SCREEN: Module 5 lesson list]

Six lessons in this module:

Lesson 1: Hallucinations — what they are and how to recognise them.
Lesson 2: Confidently wrong and quietly right — developing your judgement.
Lesson 3: Scam awareness — how AI is being used by people who want to deceive you.
Lesson 4: Privacy and Australian law — what you need to know.
Lesson 5: What never to put into an AI tool.
Lesson 6: Trust, verify, walk away — a decision framework.

[BACK TO CAMERA]

[PAUSE]

These are not abstract lessons. They are practical. By the end of this module, you will have a clear sense of what safe, confident AI use looks like — not as a list of rules, but as a set of habits that will serve you well for years.

[PAUSE]

Let's go.
