# Module 5, Lesson 4: Privacy and Australian Law

**Spoken duration:** ~10 minutes  
**Word count (spoken):** ~1,350 words  
**Format:** Direct to camera; on-screen summary points  
**On-screen text:** Key rights; practical summary  
**Tone:** Informative, calm, practical — not alarming  

---

[LOOK TO CAMERA]

Privacy is something most people want but few people understand in detail. When it comes to AI specifically, there are genuine questions worth knowing the answers to.

[PAUSE]

Where does your data go when you use an AI tool? What rights do you have? What does Australian law say? And what, in practice, should you do to protect yourself?

[PAUSE]

This lesson gives you the practical version of those answers.

[PAUSE]

[ON SCREEN: "How AI tools handle your data"]

When you type something into an AI tool and press enter, your message travels to the company's servers, is processed to generate a response, and is returned to you. What happens to it after that depends on the company and on the settings you have chosen.

[PAUSE]

Most major AI tools — including ChatGPT and Claude — collect your conversation data. By default, this data may be used to improve the AI's future responses. It is stored on servers that may be located outside Australia, typically in the United States.

[PAUSE]

This is not a secret. It is documented in each company's terms of service and privacy policy. The reason most people do not know about it is that they did not read those documents — which is entirely understandable, because they are long, technical, and not designed to be easy to read.

[PAUSE]

What this means in practical terms:

Conversations you have with AI tools may be stored.
They may be reviewed by staff at the company for safety and quality purposes.
They may be used as training data for future versions of the AI.
They are held in data centres that are subject to the laws of the country where they are located.

[PAUSE]

[ON SCREEN: "What Australian law says"]

Australia has privacy laws that regulate how organisations collect, use, store, and share personal information.

[PAUSE]

The Privacy Act 1988 is the main federal privacy law. It includes the Australian Privacy Principles, which govern how organisations with a certain annual turnover must handle personal information.

[PAUSE]

There are several things worth knowing about how Australian privacy law applies to AI tool use.

[PAUSE]

First: the Privacy Act applies to Australian organisations that are subject to it. Major international AI companies operating in Australia are generally subject to Australian privacy requirements if they are handling the data of Australians. However, enforcement across international borders is complex.

[PAUSE]

Second: you have rights under Australian law.

You have the right to know what personal information an organisation holds about you, and to request access to it.
You have the right to have inaccurate personal information corrected.
You have the right to make a complaint if you believe your privacy has been interfered with.

If you have a complaint about how an AI company has handled your personal information, the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner — OAIC — handles privacy complaints. That is oaic.gov.au.

[PAUSE]

Third: Australian health information is treated as sensitive information under Australian law and attracts stronger protections. This is one of the reasons it is particularly worth being careful about what health information you share in AI conversations.

[PAUSE]

[ON SCREEN: "What you can control"]

The most practical privacy protection is the one you control directly: what you put into the conversation.

[PAUSE]

You cannot control what happens to data once you have entered it. You can control what data you enter.

[PAUSE]

Most of the tasks we have covered in this course do not require you to enter sensitive personal information.

Drafting a letter of complaint does not require your bank account number.
Understanding how super drawdown works does not require your Tax File Number.
Summarising a document does not require you to paste the full document including your date of birth and Medicare number.
Planning a trip does not require your passport number.

[PAUSE]

In most cases, you can get the help you need from AI without sharing the most sensitive categories of information. The lesson on what never to put in — the next lesson — covers this specifically.

[PAUSE]

[ON SCREEN: "Settings worth knowing about"]

Two settings are worth being aware of in the main AI tools.

[PAUSE]

Training data opt-out.

Both ChatGPT and Claude allow you to opt out of having your conversations used as training data. We covered this in Module 1 when we set up accounts. If you have not done this, it is worth revisiting your account settings.

In ChatGPT: Settings, Data Controls, turn off "Improve the model for everyone."
In Claude: Claude's privacy settings are managed through your account. Anthropic's data practices are documented on their website.

[PAUSE]

Conversation history.

ChatGPT stores your conversations by default. You can delete individual conversations, or turn off conversation history entirely — in which case conversations are not stored. If you are using AI to discuss something sensitive, turning off history for that session is a reasonable precaution.

Claude also has settings for managing how conversations are stored.

[PAUSE]

[ON SCREEN: "A proportionate approach"]

I want to end this lesson with something practical.

[PAUSE]

Privacy concerns are legitimate. But a proportionate response to privacy risk is not avoiding AI entirely. It is making informed choices about what you share and adjusting your settings appropriately.

[PAUSE]

The people who use AI well are not people who are careless about privacy. They are people who understand the tradeoffs. They know what their data is being used for. They have turned on the settings that give them more control. They do not share information that does not need to be shared.

[PAUSE]

That is all this lesson asks of you: be informed, make sensible choices, and do not share more than the task requires.

[PAUSE]

The next lesson goes into specific detail about what never to put into an AI tool. It is the practical companion to this one.
