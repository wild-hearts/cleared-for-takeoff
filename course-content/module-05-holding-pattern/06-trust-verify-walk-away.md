# Module 5, Lesson 6: Trust, Verify, Walk Away

**Spoken duration:** ~11 minutes  
**Word count (spoken):** ~1,480 words  
**Format:** Direct to camera; on-screen framework  
**On-screen text:** The three-part decision framework; when each applies  
**Tone:** Practical, clear, leaves the learner with a usable tool  

---

[LOOK TO CAMERA]

This is the final lesson in Module 5, and it is designed to bring everything together into something you can actually use.

[PAUSE]

You now know about hallucinations. You know how to calibrate your trust in AI responses. You know the scam patterns to watch for. You know your privacy rights and what never to put in.

[PAUSE]

This lesson gives you a decision framework. Three words. Three modes.

Trust. Verify. Walk away.

[PAUSE]

[ON SCREEN: "Trust"]

Trust is the mode you use most of the time.

[PAUSE]

When you ask AI to help you draft a letter, explain how something works, summarise a document, brainstorm ideas, or plan something — you can trust the response as a useful starting point.

Not as a final authority. As a working draft, a starting explanation, a framework you then apply your own judgment to.

[PAUSE]

Trust does not mean passive acceptance. It means treating the response the way you would treat advice from a knowledgeable friend: worth taking seriously, worth reading carefully, worth adjusting based on your own situation and knowledge.

[PAUSE]

Most of what AI produces falls in the Trust category. Explanation, drafting, brainstorming, summarising, structuring — these are where AI is most consistently useful and where the risk of harm from an error is manageable.

[PAUSE]

The signal that you are in Trust mode: the task is about understanding or generating, not about relying on a specific fact or making a high-stakes decision.

[PAUSE]

[ON SCREEN: "Verify"]

Verify is the mode you shift into when the specific detail matters.

[PAUSE]

Specific figures. Named sources. Legal or medical specifics. Current eligibility rules. Time-sensitive information.

[PAUSE]

In Verify mode, you do not discard what AI has given you. You use it as the framework and context, and then check the specific details against a reliable live source before you rely on them.

[PAUSE]

The verification sources we have mentioned throughout this course:

ATO for tax information: ato.gov.au
Services Australia for payments and Medicare: servicesaustralia.gov.au
MoneySmart for financial consumer information: moneysmart.gov.au
My Aged Care for aged care: myagedcare.gov.au
NDIS: ndis.gov.au
Your state's tenancy authority for rental rights
Your super fund's own website for fund-specific information

[PAUSE]

For information about a specific law or legal right, the Australian government's legislation website — legislation.gov.au — has the actual text of Commonwealth legislation. For state laws, each state government has equivalent resources.

[PAUSE]

Verification takes two to five minutes for most tasks. The habit of opening the relevant official website after an AI response on anything significant is small effort for significant reassurance.

[PAUSE]

The signal that you are in Verify mode: AI has given you a specific figure, name, date, or procedural detail that you are going to act on, quote, or rely on.

[PAUSE]

[ON SCREEN: "Walk Away"]

Walk Away is the mode you use in specific situations that AI is genuinely not suited for.

[PAUSE]

This is not a large category. But it is an important one.

[PAUSE]

Walk Away when: the stakes are significant and the advice needs to be specific to your situation.

Major financial decisions — where to invest, how to structure your estate, whether to sell your house — require a qualified financial adviser who understands your complete situation. AI can help you prepare for that conversation. It cannot replace it.

[PAUSE]

Walk Away when: the legal consequences matter.

A contract dispute. A formal legal claim. A situation where getting the specific provision of the law wrong could have real consequences. Use AI to understand the landscape and prepare your questions. Use a solicitor for the actual advice.

[PAUSE]

Walk Away when: you are in acute medical need.

AI is useful for understanding health information, preparing for appointments, and making sense of what you have been told. It is not appropriate for diagnosing an acute situation, or as a first response to a medical emergency. For emergencies: 000. For urgent medical concerns: your GP, an urgent care clinic, or the nurse advice line on 1800 022 222.

[PAUSE]

Walk Away when: something feels off.

If a response gives you an uneasy feeling — if something seems too confident on a topic where you would expect more uncertainty, or if the information contradicts something you know from a reliable source — trust that instinct. Step away. Check elsewhere.

[PAUSE]

Walk Away is not a failure. It is a sign of good judgement.

[PAUSE]

[ON SCREEN: "Putting it together"]

Trust. Verify. Walk Away.

[PAUSE]

Most interactions: Trust.
Specific facts that matter: Verify.
High-stakes professional advice: Walk Away to a qualified professional.

[PAUSE]

Let me give you a few examples to make this concrete.

[PAUSE]

You ask AI to help you draft a letter to your council about a footpath that needs repair. Trust. The letter is not relying on specific current facts that could be wrong. AI's help here is about structure and wording.

[PAUSE]

You ask AI to explain how the age pension works in Australia and whether you might be eligible. Trust the explanation. Verify the current income and assets thresholds at servicesaustralia.gov.au. Walk Away to Centrelink for a formal assessment of your specific eligibility.

[PAUSE]

You ask AI whether a particular investment product is right for you. Walk Away. This is specific financial advice that depends on your complete financial situation. AI cannot give it. A qualified adviser can.

[PAUSE]

You receive a phone call in your grandchild's voice, saying they are in trouble and need money. Walk Away. Immediately. No AI is involved in evaluating this one — the framework is: hang up, call them directly on their actual number.

[PAUSE]

You ask AI what your rights are as a tenant when a landlord wants to end your lease. Trust the general explanation. Verify the specific legal provisions for your state with your state tenancy authority. Walk Away to a community legal centre if there is a formal dispute.

[PAUSE]

The framework is not complicated. Most situations sort themselves into the right category quickly, once you have the habit of asking the question.

[PAUSE]

[ON SCREEN: "The confident AI user"]

Let me close this module with a picture of what a confident AI user looks like.

[PAUSE]

They use AI regularly, for real tasks, without hesitation.
They read responses carefully before acting on them.
They verify specific facts when the stakes are meaningful.
They know which tasks belong with human professionals.
They are not frightened by AI, and they are not uncritically trusting of it.
They keep their sensitive personal information out of the chat.
They know what a scam looks like and they pause before they act.

[PAUSE]

That is not an aspirational picture. It is a description of what you are capable of, right now, with what you have learned in this course.

[PAUSE]

One module left. Module 6 is about the longer term — how to build a sustainable practice, stay informed without becoming anxious, and bring someone else along with you.
