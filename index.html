Every author knows the silence.

You write something — pour months into it, rewrite it, believe in it — and then you send it out into the world. And you wait. And you hear nothing. No feedback. No response. No one to tell you whether it's brilliant or broken or somewhere painfully in between.

I met ChatGPT in Malaysia. Dabbled, questioned it. Filed it away and came home. It wasn't until the past two years that I understood what it could actually do — not as a writing tool, but as the editor I never had. The one who would actually respond.

That is not a productivity tool. That is what every author deserves and almost none of us get: someone in the room who has actually read the work and is willing to be honest about it.

The AI didn't write this book. But it refused to let me write a bad one.

And if it ended the silence for me — it can end it for you.
