## Where AI helps without taking over your voice

I have written fourteen books and two hundred and sixteen songs, so let me say the scary part first. AI cannot write the thing only you can write. It does not have your childhood, your grudges, the specific way you notice a room. What it can do is take the friction out of the grind around the work, so you spend your good hours on the words that matter instead of the scaffolding. Used as a crew, not an author, here is where it genuinely earns its keep.

**Beating the blank page.** The cruellest moment is the empty document. I ask for a rough, deliberately ordinary first draft of a chapter or a verse, then I write against it. Half the time I disagree with every line, and that disagreement is the thing that gets me moving. A bad draft to react to beats a blinking cursor every time.

**Outlining and structuring.** Dump your messy notes in and ask it to suggest a shape. Where does the argument sag, what is missing, what order actually builds. You keep the through-line. It just lays the bones out so you can see them.

**A tough-but-fair editor.** Paste a passage and ask where it drags, where the meaning blurs, where you have said the same thing three ways. It will not protect your feelings the way a friend does. Take the notes that land, ignore the ones that flatten your voice.

**Brainstorming angles and titles.** Twenty title options, ten angles on the same idea, a list of what you have not considered. Most are duds. You only need the one that makes you sit up.

**Repurposing one piece into many.** A chapter becomes a newsletter, a social post, a back-cover blurb. Same heart, different lengths. This alone gives writers back hours every week.

**Research starting points.** It points you at what to look into. Then you verify every fact yourself, because it will state a wrong date with total confidence. Treat it as a lead, never a source.

Three things never leave your hands. The ideas are yours. The voice is yours. The judgement of what is good is yours. And when AI has helped in a way that matters, you say so, honestly. The assistant does not get a byline.

## Common questions about AI for writers

**Won't it make my work sound generic?**
Only if you let it do the writing. Hand it the whole job and yes, you get beige. Use it as a crew, drafting scaffolding and chasing the busywork while you keep the pen, and your voice stays yours. The generic comes from outsourcing the soul, not from using a tool.

**Is it cheating to use AI to write?**
No more than a thesaurus or a sharp editor is cheating. It is a tool. You are still the author who decides what is true, what is good, and what stays in. Where AI help genuinely shaped the work, you disclose it. Honesty is the line, not the tool itself.

**What is it actually good and bad at for creative work?**
Good at drafts to react against, structure, editing for clarity and pacing, and turning one piece into many. Bad at genuine originality and bad at facts, which it will get wrong with a straight face. The original spark and the truth-checking stay your job, always.
