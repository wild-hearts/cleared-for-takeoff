## The hats AI can take off your hands

When you run a small business, you are the sales team, the marketing department, the bookkeeper and the person who replies to the angry email at 10pm. There is no junior to hand the first draft to. That is the gap AI fills. Think of it as an unpaid junior who never sleeps and does the rough draft of almost anything, so you are left with the part only you can do: the judgement, the relationships, the call on what your business actually stands for.

I run Wild Hearts Publishing, and these are the jobs I genuinely hand over.

**Customer emails and replies.** The polite no, the refund question, the third follow-up from someone who has gone quiet. Tell it the situation in a sentence and it gives you a clear, warm reply. You read it, fix the bits that are not quite you, and send.

**Marketing and social posts.** Describe what you are launching and ask for a few caption options. It breaks the blank-screen freeze. You pick the one that sounds like you and bin the rest.

**A first draft of a proposal or quote.** Feed it your notes and it lays out a tidy structure with headings and plain wording. You put the prices in and check every line. It just saves you building the document from nothing.

**Comparing suppliers or options.** Paste two quotes or two software plans and ask what is actually different once you strip the sales talk. It lays the comparison out side by side. You still decide who you trust.

**Turning one idea into a week of content.** One decent thought becomes five posts, an email and a blog intro. Same message, different shapes, an hour instead of an afternoon.

**Drafting policies, FAQs and long-document summaries.** A returns policy, a set of FAQs, or a plain summary of a fourteen-page contract so you know what you are signing. A starting point, not the final word.

Be honest about the limits. It does not know your customers, your numbers or your market. It does not make the business calls, and it will state things with confidence that are simply wrong, so you check. What it clears is the admin and the blank pages, which is most of what steals your evenings.

## Common questions about AI for small business

**What should I hand to AI first?**
Start with the repetitive writing: customer emails and social posts. It is the lowest-risk work, because you read every word before it goes out, and it gives the fastest payback. You will feel the hours come back in the first week. Once that feels natural, move on to proposals, FAQs and the longer drafts.

**Do I need to pay for it?**
No, not to start. The free versions of the main tools are genuinely good enough for emails, posts, drafts and summaries, and plenty of owners never pay a cent. The paid plans exist for heavy daily use and a few extra features. Start free, and only upgrade if you actually hit a wall, which most small businesses do not.

**Will customers know it's AI?**
Not if you do it properly, because you are still the one talking to them. AI gives you a draft, never the final word. You edit it into your own voice, cut anything that sounds generic, and add the detail only you know. The customer hears you, because the words that leave your business are the ones you chose to keep.
