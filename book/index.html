Every author knows the silence.

You write something. Pour months into it. Rewrite it. Believe in it. Then you send it out into the world. And you wait. And you hear nothing. No feedback. No response. No one to tell you whether it's brilliant or broken or somewhere painfully in between.

I met ChatGPT in Malaysia. Dabbled, questioned it. Filed it away and came home. It wasn't until the past two years that I understood what it could actually do. Not as a writing tool. As the editor I never had. The one who would actually respond.

That is not a productivity tool. That is what every author deserves and almost none of us get: someone in the room who has actually read the work and is willing to be honest about it.

The AI didn't write this book. But it refused to let me write a bad one.

And if it ended the silence for me, it can end it for you.

About the Author

Naomi Shiels is a Canberra-based former linguist, lawyer and commercial aviation manager. She is the founder of Wild Hearts Publishing. She has written 14 books. She has made 216 songs using AI. She builds with these tools every day.

She is not an engineer. She did not go to a tech conference and come home converted. She learned to use AI the same way you will: by showing up, trying things, failing at some of them, and keeping what worked.

This book is what she kept.
