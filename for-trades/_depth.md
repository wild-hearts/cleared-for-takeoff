## Where AI actually helps on the tools

You did not get into the trade to spend Sunday night writing quotes. But that is where a lot of nights go. AI will not run a cable, set a tile, or decide what a job is worth. What it does is the writing and the chasing, so the part that pays you stays the part you do, and the admin stops eating your evenings.

Here is where it genuinely earns its keep.

**Turning a voice note into a tidy quote.** Standing in the driveway after a site visit, you talk into your phone: replace the meter box, run a new sub-main, supply and fit four downlights, half a day's work. Hand AI that ramble and it gives you back a clean, itemised quote in plain English. You put the prices in. You check every line. It just saves you typing the wording from scratch at 9pm.

**Drafting variations and scope changes.** The client wants the extra power point now they have seen the wall. AI writes the variation note clearly, so there is a written record before you do the work, not an awkward conversation about it after.

**Chasing the invoice that is three weeks overdue.** You do not want to be rude and you do not want to be a pushover. Ask for a firm-but-fair reminder and you get one. Polite, dated, asks for payment, no waffle. Send it and get on with your day.

**Quick client texts and updates.** Running late because the supplier let you down? "Write a short, friendly text telling the Hendersons I am held up and will be there by two." Done in seconds, and it reads like a professional, not a bloke who can't be bothered.

**A first draft of a safety document.** AI can rough out a SWMS or a method statement from your notes. Read it. It is a starting point, not a sign-off. The compliance call is yours, every time.

**Comparing two supplier quotes.** Paste both, ask what is actually different once you strip the formatting. It lays out the comparison. You still decide who you trust to deliver.

**A Facebook post for the business.** Finished a good bathroom? Describe it, ask for a short post for the page. Photos are yours; the caption takes thirty seconds instead of fifteen minutes of staring at the screen.

None of this touches the trade, the pricing judgement, or the compliance sign-off. That stays with you, where it belongs. It just hands you back the admin hours.

## Common questions about AI for tradies

**Can AI write my quotes for me?**
It writes the wording, fast, from a voice note or a few bullet points. It does not set the prices and it does not know your margins. You put the numbers in and read it before it goes out. Think of it as the bloke who types it up neatly while you make the calls that matter.

**Is it hard to learn if I'm not techy?**
No. It is plain typing, the same as texting. You open a website, type what you want in normal words, and it answers. There is nothing to install if you do not want to, no settings to wrestle with, and no wrong way to ask. If you can send a text from the ute, you can do this.

**Will it cost a fortune?**
No. The free versions are genuinely good enough for quotes, reminders, texts, and posts. Plenty of tradies never pay a cent. The paid plans exist for heavy daily use, but start free and only think about upgrading if you actually hit a limit, which most sole operators never do.
