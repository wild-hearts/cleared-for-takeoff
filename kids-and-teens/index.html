What they learn

Ten weeks, week by week

Each week is one short video lesson with the animated instructor, then a hands-on activity to do at home. Here is the full flight plan for both tracks. Week 1 of each is a free preview.

Junior AI Navigators

Ages 8 to 11. Earns the AI Flight Log, Level 1.

  1. 1What is AI?
    Say what AI is, and what it is not
  2. 2Talking to AI safely
    Clear instructions, and staying safe
  3. 3Machines that see
    What makes a good example for a computer
  4. 4Train your first classifier
    Teach a computer to tell two things apart
  5. 5When AI gets it wrong
    Mistakes, fairness, and why data matters
  6. 6AI storytelling
    Co-write a story, then make it yours
  7. 7Scratch helpers
    Build a tiny game with a smart rule
  8. 8Safe and smart online
    Safe sharing, and checking if AI is right
  9. 9Build week
    Plan and build a project of their own
  10. 10Showcase week
    Present it, and earn their wings

Senior AI Pilots

Ages 12 to 16. Earns the AI Co-Pilot License, Level 1.

  1. 1How AI systems work
    Trace data to output: predict, generate, classify
  2. 2Prompt design and evaluation
    Design strong prompts, and build a rubric
  3. 3Data and labelling
    Build a balanced dataset, and document it
  4. 4Building an image model
    Train, improve it, and read the thresholds
  5. 5Bias, fairness and failure
    Audit for skew, and write a model card
  6. 6AI-assisted storytelling and media
    A branching piece, edited in their own voice
  7. 7Human-centred design
    Pitch an AI solution, with safeguards
  8. 8Ethics, privacy and copyright
    Consent, deepfakes, ownership, and when humans decide
  9. 9Capstone sprint
    Build one substantial project
  10. 10Demo day and reflection
    Present, defend the choices, and earn the licence

For parents

Built for young people, designed for parents

This is the part we think about most. The course is taught by an animated instructor, not a live stranger, and there is no open chat for children to wander into. You hold the account, you give consent, and you can see what your child is learning. The aim is a confident, sensible young person who understands AI, not one who is hooked on it.

An animated instructor

Lessons are pre-made videos with a friendly animated guide. No live calls, no strangers, no open chat rooms.

Parents in command

You create and hold the account and give consent before your child starts. Their privacy is the default, not an afterthought.

Plain English, age-appropriate

Every lesson is pitched to the age group, using tools made for classrooms. Learning to think, not just to click.

Optional flight routes

Choose a direction to specialise

After the core track, pilots can choose a route that matches what they love. Each route adds a short set of projects on top of the credential.

Creative route

Stories, art, music and design with AI as a helper, keeping the young maker firmly in charge of the ideas.

Safety & ethics route

Deepfakes, scams, bias and fairness. How to question what you see online and use these tools honestly.

Data & coding route

A gentle first look at data and simple code, building and testing their own small models step by step.

Routes are optional. The core ten-week track and its credential come first; routes are there for pilots who want to go further.

Wings to keep

  • AI Co-Pilot License – Level 1 for Senior Pilots who finish a real project
