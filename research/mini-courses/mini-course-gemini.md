# Google Gemini: Your AI That Lives Inside Google

**Course summary:** A five-lesson mini-course on Google Gemini for Australian adults aged 50+. Designed for people who already use Gmail, Google Photos, or an Android phone. Focuses on the unique Google ecosystem integration that makes Gemini different from other AI tools. Suitable for NotebookLM, video, and podcast format.

This course is for anyone who uses Google products as part of their daily life — Gmail for email, Google Photos for pictures, Google Calendar for appointments, Google Maps for directions, or any Android phone. You do not need prior AI experience. If you have tried ChatGPT and are wondering whether Gemini is different — this course answers that question directly.

## Lesson 1: What Is Gemini and Why Should You Care?

### Running time: approximately 10 minutes

Google Gemini is Google's AI assistant. It is the AI layer built on top of everything Google already knows and offers.

Google has been the world's most-used search engine for over 25 years. If you have been using the internet for any length of time, you have used Google to find things. Gemini is what happens when Google's search capability is combined with a conversational AI — something that can not only find information but synthesise it into a clear answer for you.

But Gemini's more interesting capability is the integration with your personal Google services.

Imagine being able to ask: "What was the name of that physiotherapist I emailed about my knee last March?" And receiving the answer immediately from your own Gmail, without having to scroll through months of emails. Or: "Find all the photos I took at my granddaughter's school concert in 2024." And watching Google Photos retrieve them from whatever folder they ended up in.

That is the promise of Gemini in its integrated form. It connects your AI assistant to your actual life — your appointments, your emails, your photos, your documents — in a way that no other AI tool can, because no other AI tool has Google's infrastructure behind it.

**The tradeoff.** This integration requires giving Google's AI access to your personal data. Google already has this data — when you use Gmail, Google has your emails; when you use Google Photos, Google has your photos. Gemini bringing AI to bear on this data is a feature if you trust Google with it, and a privacy concern if you do not. We will address this honestly in Lesson 4.

**The practical appeal.** For anyone who finds their inbox overwhelming, who has years of photos in Google Photos that they cannot find, or who wants to search the current web rather than relying on an AI's potentially dated knowledge — Gemini offers distinct advantages.

## Lesson 2: Setting It Up — Step by Step

### Running time: approximately 8 minutes

There are three ways to use Gemini, and you may want to use all three depending on what you need.

### Option 1: The Gemini Website (any computer)

1. Go to **https://gemini.google.com**
2. If you are signed in to Google in your browser, you will go straight to Gemini. If not, click "Sign in" and enter your Gmail address and password.
3. You will see a chat interface. Type a question and press Enter.
4. Gemini responds with a synthesis of information, typically with source links at the bottom.

**This works without any special integration** — Gemini is just answering from Google Search and its AI model. This is a good way to start.

### Option 2: Gemini Inside Gmail

1. Open Gmail in your browser (mail.google.com)
2. Look for a sparkle icon (✦) in the right-hand sidebar — this is Gemini's handle inside Gmail.
3. Click it. A Gemini panel opens on the right side of your Gmail.
4. Ask it to "Summarise my last 10 unread emails" or "Find emails from [name] about [topic]."
5. To enable this feature, you may need to allow Gemini access to Gmail when prompted. This is where the privacy consideration begins — see Lesson 4.

### Option 3: On an Android Phone (Google Assistant or Gemini App)

1. On most Android phones, hold down the home button or say "Hey Google" — this activates Google's AI features.
2. Alternatively, download the "Gemini" app from the Google Play Store.
3. Open the app. You can type or speak your question.
4. Using the phone camera: in the Google app (search bar at the top of your home screen), tap the camera icon. This is Google Lens. Point at anything — a plant, a product, a menu in a foreign language, a sign — and ask questions about what you see.

### On iPhone

Google offers the Gemini app on iPhone via the App Store. Search "Gemini — Google AI." The Gmail integration is more limited on iPhone than on Android.

## Lesson 3: Your First Five Conversations

### Running time: approximately 15 minutes

Gemini's best conversations tend to be ones where you are using Google's search integration, or where you are working within the Google ecosystem. Keep this in mind as we try these examples.

---

### Conversation 1: Current research

Gemini's web search integration makes it better than competitors for current information.

> **"I'm trying to understand the current situation with the National Disability Insurance Scheme and how recent changes might affect people who help care for a family member with a disability. Can you give me an up-to-date summary?"**

Gemini will search the current web and synthesise a response with links to sources. Review the sources — Gemini tends to surface government websites and reputable news sources for this kind of query.

---

### Conversation 2: Gmail management

If you have enabled Gemini inside Gmail:

> **"I just got back from two weeks away and I have 94 unread emails. Can you go through them and tell me: which ones need a response, which ones contain important information I should know about, and which ones are safe to delete or archive?"**

Gemini will read your emails and give you a summary. This is the feature many Gmail users call transformative once they try it.

---

### Conversation 3: Google Photos search

In the Gemini app on Android (or via google.com/photos):

> **"I'm looking for photos from my grandson's birthday party. I think it was in August 2023, but I'm not completely sure. Can you find them?"**

Or, directly in Google Photos, use the search bar: "Birthday party 2023" — Google Photos now uses AI to search by content, not just filename.

---

### Conversation 4: Understanding something in real time with the camera

On your Android phone, open Google Lens (the camera icon in the Google Search bar).

Try these real-life uses:
- Point at a plant you do not recognise and ask "What is this plant and is it native to Australia?"
- Point at a medication bottle and ask "What is this medication typically used for and what are the common side effects?"
- Point at a warning light on your car dashboard and ask "What does this warning light mean?"
- Point at a label in a foreign language and ask "What does this say?"

Google Lens with Gemini's AI behind it makes your phone camera a remarkably useful research tool.

---

### Conversation 5: Trip planning with current information

> **"I'm planning a trip from Brisbane to Cairns by car in August. My partner and I are both in our 70s, we like wildlife, great food, and easy walking — nothing too strenuous. We want to take about 12 days. Can you suggest a route with current information about must-see stops, where to stay, and approximate costs?"**

Gemini searches for current information rather than relying only on training data, which means accommodation recommendations and cost estimates are more likely to be current than with a pure AI assistant.

## Lesson 4: The Things Nobody Tells You

### Running time: approximately 10 minutes

**The Google data question requires a direct answer.** When you use Gmail, Google has your emails. When you use Google Photos, Google has your photos. Google's advertising business is built on using what it knows about you to show you relevant ads. When you enable Gemini to access your Gmail or Google Photos, you are giving AI-powered analysis access to some of the most personal data you have.

This does not mean you should not do it. But you should make this choice consciously, not accidentally.

If you use Google products and are comfortable with the data arrangement already (which many people are — Gmail has been reading emails for spam detection for years), then Gemini integration adds significant useful functionality for an equivalent level of trust.

If the idea of your emails being analysed by AI makes you uncomfortable, you can use Gemini at gemini.google.com for general questions without enabling the Gmail or Photos integration. You still get a capable AI assistant; you just don't get the personalised features.

**Gemini's Workspace integration is being expanded.** In 2025 and 2026, Google has been adding Gemini capabilities to more Google products. Features change frequently; what is described in this course reflects the state of the product as of May 2026.

**Accuracy is not guaranteed.** Despite the web search integration, Gemini can still produce incorrect information. The sources it cites help you verify — use them.

**The naming is confusing.** "Gemini" appears as a standalone website, as a feature in Gmail, as a phone assistant, as a business tool (Google Workspace), and in Google Search itself. Each behaves slightly differently and has slightly different capabilities. If something seems not to work, it may be that you are using a different version of Gemini than the one being described.

**Australian privacy law and Google.** Google has an Australian operation and is subject to Australian privacy law. It has an Australian privacy policy and processes some Australian data locally. The OAIC has oversight of Google's Australian operations. This gives Australian users a somewhat stronger privacy position than users in some other countries.

## Lesson 5: Making It Part of Your Life

### Running time: approximately 8 minutes

The path to making Gemini useful depends entirely on where you live in the digital world.

**If you are a heavy Gmail user,** the single highest-value change you can make is enabling Gemini in Gmail and spending 10 minutes letting it summarise your inbox. Many people who do this never look back. The time saved on email alone justifies the Google integration.

**If you have years of photos in Google Photos,** start experimenting with the AI search feature. Search for events by description, not filename. "Family Christmas 2019." "Trip to New Zealand." "Mum's 80th birthday." You may be surprised at how well it works.

**If you have an Android phone,** the camera/Lens feature is worth building into your daily curiosity. See something you don't recognise? Point your camera at it and ask. This transforms everyday curiosity into instant knowledge.

**If you are primarily a computer user,** start at gemini.google.com for general research. Gemini is particularly good for questions requiring current information — what is happening with a particular policy, what is the current price range for something, what do current reviews say about a service.

**A morning habit.** Some people start their day by asking Gemini "What's happening in Australian news today that I should know about?" and "Do I have anything important in my calendar this week?" Two questions, two answers, and you are oriented for the day in about two minutes.

## Quick Reference Card

### Core Gemini Uses

**For current research:**
> "Can you search for current information about [topic] and give me a summary with sources?"

**For Gmail:**
> "Summarise my unread emails from the past week. Flag anything that needs a response."

**For Google Photos:**
> [In Google Photos search bar] "Photos of [person/event/place] from [year]"

**For the camera/Lens:**
> [Point camera at object] "What is this?" or "What does this say?" or "Is this plant safe for dogs?"

**For travel:**
> "I'm planning to visit [destination] in [month]. I want [preferences]. What are the current must-dos and how much should I budget for [accommodation/food/activities]?"

---

### Controlling Your Privacy

- **To disable Gemini Apps Activity** (stops Google using your Gemini conversations for improvement): Go to gemini.google.com > Settings > Gemini Apps Activity > Turn off
- **To remove Gmail integration:** In Gmail settings, find the Gemini/AI features section and revoke access
- **To review what Google knows about you:** Go to myaccount.google.com — one of the most comprehensive data dashboards of any major technology company
