# Canva AI: Making Beautiful Things, Even If You Can't Draw

**Course summary:** A five-lesson mini-course on Canva's AI features (Magic Studio) for Australian adults aged 50+. Canva is the beloved Australian design app, and Magic Studio adds AI to make visual creation even easier. Covers setup, key AI features, creative projects, and five hands-on activities. Suitable for NotebookLM, video, and podcast format.

## Who This Course Is For

This course is for anyone who has ever wanted to create something beautiful — a card, a flyer, a photo book, a presentation — but felt they lacked the artistic or technical skill to do it.

It is also for anyone who already uses Canva and would like to discover what its AI features can do.

No design experience needed. No drawing ability needed. You do not need to understand colour theory or typography. Canva and its AI tools do that work for you.

## Lesson 1: What Is Canva AI and Why Should You Care?

### Running time: approximately 10 minutes

Canva is an Australian company. It was founded in Perth in 2012, is now headquartered in Sydney, and has become one of the most successful technology companies Australia has produced.

The original idea was simple: professional design is too hard for most people because the tools (Photoshop, InDesign) require years of training. Canva built design software that anyone can use — templates, drag-and-drop, intuitive tools — so a parent making a birthday invitation, a volunteer running a community event, or a small business owner making a Facebook post could do it themselves in minutes.

That idea worked. Canva now has over 200 million users worldwide.

Magic Studio is the name Canva gives to its AI features. And they are significant.

**What the AI adds:**

Think about what makes design difficult. You need to find the right images. You need to know what looks good together. You need to write words that fit the space. You need to know what to do when something is not working.

Magic Studio addresses each of these:
- **No image? Generate one.** Dream Lab creates original artwork from a text description.
- **Photo background is cluttered? Remove it.** Background Remover makes your subject the sole focus in seconds.
- **Something ruining your photo? Erase it.** Magic Eraser paints over any element and fills it seamlessly.
- **Not sure what to say? AI writes it.** Magic Write generates captions, headlines, and short copy.
- **Not sure how to start? AI designs it.** Magic Design takes your content and generates a layout for you.

**Why does this matter for someone aged 50+?**

Because the desire to create beautiful things and the skill to do so have always had a gap between them. Canva, and now Canva AI, bridges that gap.

You can now:
- Create a family history book that looks like a professional publication
- Design a personalised card for every significant occasion
- Make a stunning tribute poster for a loved one's funeral
- Produce a community newsletter that people actually want to read
- Generate original artwork to print and hang on your wall

And you can do all of this without any design training, without expensive software, and (for many tasks) without spending a cent.

## Lesson 2: Setting It Up — Step by Step

### Running time: approximately 8 minutes

### Creating Your Account

1. Go to **https://www.canva.com**
2. Click "Sign up" — the simplest method is to click "Continue with Google" if you use Gmail, or "Continue with Apple" if you use Apple products. This saves creating a new password.
3. Alternatively, click "Continue with email" and enter your email address to create an account.
4. Canva will ask what you are designing for — select the option that fits (Personal is fine for most people). You do not need to share this information if you prefer.
5. You arrive at the Canva home screen.

### Finding Your Way Around the Home Screen

The Canva home screen has:
- A search bar at the top: "What will you design today?"
- Below it: category tiles — Social media, Presentations, Cards, Videos, Print, and more
- On the left: "Home," "Recent," "Projects," and options to start a new design

Start by clicking on a category that interests you — "Cards" for a greeting card, "Presentations" for a slideshow, "Photo Books" for a family history project.

### Where to Find AI Features

AI features are built into the editing interface, not the home screen. Once you are in a design, you access them via:
- The **toolbar** at the top: look for a sparkle icon (✦)
- The **left sidebar**: apps and elements include AI-powered options
- **Clicking on a photo** in your design: reveals AI editing options (Background Remover, Magic Eraser, Magic Edit, Magic Expand)
- **Clicking on a text box**: reveals Magic Write

### Free vs Pro Features

On the free plan:
- 50 AI credits per month (shared across Magic Write and image generation)
- Background Remover: 5 uses per month
- Basic Magic Design

On Pro (AUD $24.99/month or AUD $169.99/year):
- 500 AI credits per month
- Unlimited Background Remover
- Full Dream Lab (image generation)
- Full Magic Eraser, Magic Edit, Magic Expand
- 130+ million premium assets

Start with the free plan. If you find you are regularly hitting the credit limit, Pro is worth the annual subscription.

## Lesson 3: Your First Five Creations

### Running time: approximately 15 minutes

---

### Creation 1: A personalised birthday card

1. On the Canva home screen, type "Birthday card" in the search bar and press Enter
2. Browse the templates. Click on one that appeals.
3. The editor opens. Click on any text to edit it — change the name, the age, the message.
4. To generate AI text: click on a text box, look for the sparkle icon, click it, and type: "A warm, slightly funny birthday message for a friend turning 65 who loves gardening and red wine." Click Insert.
5. To change the photo if there is one: click on the image, then the three dots, and choose "Replace." Upload a photo from your computer, or choose from Canva's free image library.
6. When you are happy, click "Share" (top right) and choose "Download." Your card is ready to print or send digitally.

---

### Creation 2: Removing a background from a photo

This is one of Canva's most useful features, and it takes about 10 seconds.

1. In Canva, start a new design (any size — Instagram Post is a good canvas)
2. Click the image icon in the left sidebar and upload a photo of a person you would like to extract from its background
3. The photo appears in your design. Click on it.
4. In the toolbar that appears at the top, click "Edit Photo" or look for "Remove Background"
5. Click "Remove Background." Watch the background disappear.
6. You now have just the subject — on a transparent background that you can place on any new background you choose

Practical uses: Extracting a photo of a person to use in a memory collage. Taking a product photo and removing the cluttered background. Creating a personalised illustration by placing a photo against a painted or patterned background.

---

### Creation 3: Generating an original image with Dream Lab

1. In any Canva design, click "Apps" in the left sidebar
2. Find "Dream Lab" — it may also appear via the sparkle icon in the image toolbar
3. Type a description of the image you want:
   > "A delicate watercolour illustration of Australian native flowers — waratah, kangaroo paw, and wattle — arranged as a loose bouquet on a white background, botanical art style"
4. Click "Generate." In 10-20 seconds, four image options appear.
5. Click the one you like to add it to your design.

Dream Lab is particularly good for:
- Floral and botanical illustrations
- Australian landscapes and scenes
- Decorative patterns and backgrounds
- Portrait-style illustrations (though very specific faces are difficult)

---

### Creation 4: Writing a caption with Magic Write

1. In any design with a text box, click the text box
2. Look for the Magic Write option (usually a sparkle icon or "Ask AI" button)
3. Type your request:
   > "Write a warm message for a card celebrating the retirement of a nurse who has spent 35 years working in paediatrics. About 30-40 words. Tone: heartfelt and proud."
4. Click Generate. Review the result. If you like it, click Insert. If not, click Generate again for a different version.

---

### Creation 5: Erasing something from a photo

1. Upload a photo to Canva that has something you would like to remove — a telephone pole, a rubbish bin, an unwanted background element
2. Click the photo in your design
3. In the edit toolbar, find "Magic Eraser" (in Pro) or "Edit Photo > Eraser"
4. Use the brush to paint over what you want to remove
5. Click "Erase." The AI fills in the gap with the surrounding background.

This works best for:
- Removing objects from natural backgrounds (grass, sky, sand)
- Removing small or medium-sized elements
- Photos where the background is relatively uniform near the element you are removing

## Lesson 4: The Things Nobody Tells You

### Running time: approximately 10 minutes

**AI credits run out.** On the free plan, 50 AI credits sounds like a lot — but generating a single Dream Lab image typically uses 1-4 credits, and if you are using Magic Write and editing features as well, you can burn through 50 credits in a creative session. Manage them thoughtfully, or upgrade to Pro.

**Dream Lab is better at some things than others.** It produces excellent: landscapes, botanical illustrations, abstract art, atmospheric scenes, painterly styles. It is less reliable for: photorealistic people, text within images, very specific objects or places. Know its sweet spots.

**Text on designs is separate from AI.** Dream Lab can create images, but it cannot reliably put legible text inside an image. If you want a poster with words on it, generate the image and then add text as a separate text layer in Canva. This is always the right approach.

**Print quality.** Canva's download quality for print purposes is good but not always perfect for very large prints. For standard card or A4 document printing, it is fine. For large-format printing (posters larger than A3), check the resolution settings before downloading.

**Canva is Australian — this matters for support.** Canva has Australian customer support. If you have trouble, their help centre (canva.com/help) is thorough, and support staff operate in Australian business hours. This is a meaningful advantage over US-based tools.

**Privacy considerations.** Photos you upload to Canva are stored on Canva's servers. Canva may use your content and usage data to improve its AI features. If you are uploading particularly personal or sensitive photos, be aware of this. You can delete designs and uploaded media from your account at any time.

## Lesson 5: Making It Part of Your Life

### Running time: approximately 8 minutes

The best way to make Canva AI part of your life is to find your personal use case — the thing you do regularly that Canva makes dramatically easier or better.

**For people who communicate with groups** — church, bowls club, U3A, neighbourhood association, community choir — Canva becomes the newsletter and poster tool. Once you have a template you like, creating new issues or posters takes 20 minutes instead of two hours.

**For people who care about personal expression** — cards for every occasion, photo gifts for family, personalised artwork for the home — Canva becomes the creative workshop. Instead of buying generic cards, you create ones that are specifically, unmistakably for the person receiving them.

**For people undertaking legacy projects** — family histories, memoir preparation, tribute books — Canva is the publishing tool. The book template feature lets you design something that looks like a real publication. With AI generating images for illustration, it becomes achievable by anyone.

**A monthly creative session.** Consider setting aside 30-60 minutes once a month for Canva. Use it to create something specific — the upcoming birthday cards for the month, a design for a community post, an update to the family newsletter. Consistent use builds skill faster than occasional dipping in.
