# Midjourney: Making Art With Words

**Course summary:** A five-lesson mini-course on Midjourney for Australian adults aged 50+. Midjourney is the AI image generation tool that produces genuinely beautiful art from text descriptions. Designed for anyone with a creative eye who wants to generate original visual art without technical skill. Suitable for NotebookLM, video, and podcast format.

---

## Who This Course Is For

This course is for anyone who has always had a visual imagination but perhaps not the technical skills to realise it.

You do not need to draw. You do not need to know Photoshop. You do not need any design training. You need words — the ability to describe what you see in your mind — and Midjourney provides the rest.

This course is also for anyone working on creative or personal projects: family histories, self-published books, cards and prints, community publications, or simply the pleasure of creating art.

---

## What You Will Be Able to Do by the End

- Access Midjourney via its web interface (no Discord required for basic use)
- Write prompts that produce high-quality, intentional images
- Develop variations on images you like
- Download and use your images for personal projects
- Understand the difference between what Midjourney does well and what it struggles with

## Lesson 1: What Is Midjourney and Why Should You Care?

### Running time: approximately 10 minutes

There is an old wish that runs through a lot of creative lives: if only I could paint what I see in my mind.

For centuries, turning mental images into visual art required either developing a physical skill over years or paying someone else to do it. Neither option was accessible to most people.

Midjourney is not quite the full answer to that wish — it does not give you total control over every element of the image, and learning to get consistently good results takes time. But it is closer than anything that has existed before.

You type: "A golden retriever asleep in autumn leaves, afternoon light through trees, oil painting style, warm palette." You wait thirty seconds. And you see four versions of that image — each one a complete, genuinely artistic interpretation of those words.

Some are exactly what you imagined. Some are slightly different. Some are surprisingly better than what you imagined. And from here, you can develop them further: ask for a variation that is slightly different, upscale the one you like best, or use it as a starting point for something completely new.

This is not just a novelty. Real creative work is being done with Midjourney: book illustrations, cover art, wall prints, greeting cards, community publications, family history illustrations, memorial tributes, and personal creative exploration.

**Who makes it.** Midjourney was founded in 2022 by David Holz, who previously co-founded Leap Motion (a hand-tracking technology company). Midjourney operates as a small, independent company in San Francisco and has remained privately held while building what many consider the best AI art generator available.

**What makes it different from Canva Dream Lab or ChatGPT images.** Those tools produce capable images. Midjourney produces art. The difference is aesthetic quality, compositional sophistication, and a certain visual intelligence that other tools have not yet matched. If you care about how beautiful the image is, Midjourney wins.

## Lesson 2: Setting It Up — Step by Step

### Running time: approximately 8 minutes

Midjourney's setup requires one extra step: you need a Discord account. Discord is a chat and community platform, originally built for gamers but now used widely. Do not let this put you off — the Discord part is brief and straightforward.

### Step 1: Create a Discord Account

1. Go to **https://discord.com**
2. Click "Register" or "Open Discord in your browser"
3. Enter a username, your email address, and a password
4. Verify your email by clicking the link Discord sends you
5. You may be asked to verify you are a human by completing a brief puzzle

You now have a Discord account. You do not need to do anything else in Discord directly.

### Step 2: Create a Midjourney Account

1. Go to **https://www.midjourney.com**
2. Click "Sign in" or "Get Started"
3. When prompted, authorise Midjourney to connect to your Discord account
4. You are now signed in to Midjourney's web interface

### Step 3: Subscribe to a Plan

The free trial is very limited. To generate meaningful numbers of images, a paid plan is required.

1. On the Midjourney website, click your account icon (top right)
2. Select "Subscribe"
3. Choose your plan — the Basic plan (USD $10/month, approximately AUD $16/month) is the right starting point
4. Enter your payment details

### Step 4: Generate Your First Image

1. You are on the Midjourney web interface. Look for the text box at the bottom of the screen — this is your prompt box.
2. Type your first description. Something straightforward:
   > "Australian bush in early morning light, eucalyptus trees, mist, golden glow, watercolour painting style"
3. Press Enter. Four image variations appear in about 30-60 seconds.
4. Click on any image to view it larger.

## Lesson 3: Your First Five Creations

### Running time: approximately 15 minutes

The skill in Midjourney is in the prompt — your text description. These examples are designed to get you producing good results immediately.

---

### Creation 1: An Australian landscape

Start with a landscape — the widest, most forgiving subject in Midjourney.

> **"Sunset over the Australian outback, red earth, sparse eucalyptus trees, vast open sky in deep orange and purple, painterly oil painting style, wide angle, moody and atmospheric"**

Press Enter. Review the four results. Click on the one you like. Below the image, you will see buttons: "Vary (Subtle)" for minor variations, "Vary (Strong)" for more different interpretations, and "Upscale" to get a high-resolution version suitable for printing.

---

### Creation 2: A botanical illustration

Botanical illustrations are one of Midjourney's strongest capabilities. Perfect for cards, gift wraps, and home prints.

> **"Detailed botanical illustration of Australian native plants — waratah, banksia, and eucalyptus — in the style of 19th century scientific illustration, pen and ink with watercolour, white background, fine detail, elegant"**

Review the four results. The level of detail and elegance in these illustrations often surprises first-time Midjourney users.

---

### Creation 3: A portrait scene for a family history

If you are working on a family history and want an illustration of a scene from the past:

> **"A family gathered around a kitchen table in a farmhouse in rural Australia in the 1940s, warm lamp light, children doing homework, parents talking, wood stove in the background, black and white photograph style, historical, intimate"**

This kind of historical, atmospheric scene is where Midjourney particularly shines. The "black and white photograph style" instruction often produces especially convincing results.

---

### Creation 4: A personalised gift or card image

> **"A charming cottage garden in summer, English cottage aesthetic, roses climbing a white fence, lavender and delphiniums, butterflies, golden afternoon light, soft focus, watercolour illustration style, suitable for a greeting card"**

Download this image (right-click > Save Image, or use the download button) and import it into Canva to add text for a card or gift tag.

---

### Creation 5: Creative exploration

This one is for pure creative play. Describe something you have always had in your visual imagination:

> **"A woman sits at a writing desk in a room overlooking the sea, late afternoon, handwritten letters spread across the desk, a cup of tea, quiet contemplation, soft light through lace curtains, impressionist painting style, warm and nostalgic"**

Review the results. Notice what Midjourney interpreted differently from what you imagined. Ask for a variation. Explore.

## Lesson 4: The Things Nobody Tells You

### Running time: approximately 10 minutes

**The learning curve is real but worthwhile.** Your first few Midjourney sessions may produce results that are not quite what you hoped for. This is normal. Midjourney rewards learning — specifically, learning how to describe what you want more precisely. After a few sessions, the difference in quality is significant.

**Prompting tips that immediately improve results:**
- Specify the **art style** ("oil painting," "watercolour," "pencil sketch," "photography," "illustration")
- Specify the **lighting** ("golden hour," "soft diffused light," "dramatic contrast," "morning mist")
- Specify the **mood** ("peaceful," "melancholic," "joyful," "contemplative")
- Specify the **perspective** ("close-up," "wide angle," "aerial view," "portrait orientation")
- Add "high quality, detailed" for more refined results

**Text in images — a warning.** Midjourney struggles with rendering legible text inside images. If you want an image with words on it — a poster with a heading, a card with a message — generate the image without text in Midjourney, then add the text as a layer in Canva. This is always the right approach.

**Hands are still imperfect.** AI image generation, including Midjourney, sometimes produces hands with unusual detail or extra fingers. This is a known limitation being improved but not yet solved. If you need clear hands in an image, check carefully and regenerate if needed.

**The public gallery.** By default, images you generate on the Basic and Standard plans appear in Midjourney's public Explore gallery — other users can see your creations. If privacy is important, the Pro plan ($60/month) includes Stealth Mode.

**Commercial use.** On any paid plan, you own the images you generate and can use them commercially. If you plan to sell products featuring Midjourney images, this is your legal basis. Read the current terms of service for the details.

**Discord is minimal.** You set it up once and do not need to use Discord again for the web interface. The web interface at midjourney.com is the place to generate images. Discord is just the login mechanism.

## Lesson 5: Making It Part of Your Life

### Running time: approximately 8 minutes

Midjourney is a creative tool, which means its place in your life depends on where creativity sits for you.

**If you are working on a specific project** — a family history book, a collection of cards, illustrations for a memoir — Midjourney becomes a production tool. Set aside regular sessions for the project and develop a consistent style across your images by using the same style descriptors in each prompt.

**If you are exploring creativity for its own sake** — Midjourney can become a creative practice. Spend 30 minutes a week exploring a theme, a period of history, a landscape, or a feeling. Save the images that move you. Develop an aesthetic sense through iteration and exploration.

**For gift-giving** — Midjourney plus Canva is a powerful combination. Generate original artwork in Midjourney. Import into Canva. Add text for a card. Print at home or through a print service. The result is a personalised gift that costs a fraction of what professional custom artwork would.

**Building your prompting vocabulary.** The art of good Midjourney prompting is essentially the art of visual description. If you want to improve: look at paintings and illustrations you admire, and practise describing them in words. Then use those descriptions in Midjourney and see how close you get. This is both a creative exercise and a skill-building one.

## Quick Reference Card

### Prompt Structure That Works

**[Subject] + [Setting/Context] + [Mood/Atmosphere] + [Art Style] + [Lighting]**

Example:
> "An elderly man reading on a beach [subject], at dawn with soft fog over the water [setting], peaceful and contemplative [mood], impressionist oil painting [art style], warm golden light [lighting]"

---

### Shortcut Words That Improve Results

**Art styles:** oil painting, watercolour, pencil sketch, charcoal, linocut, botanical illustration, impressionist, photorealistic, anime, Art Nouveau, vintage photograph

**Lighting:** golden hour, soft diffused light, dramatic side light, morning mist, candlelight, dappled forest light, studio lighting

**Quality boosters:** highly detailed, masterful, beautiful composition, award-winning, cinematic

**Australian specifics:** Australian native flora, bush landscape, red earth, gum tree, eucalyptus, Southern Ocean, outback

---

### After You Get an Image

- **Vary Subtle:** Minor changes, same overall image
- **Vary Strong:** More different variations, same concept
- **Upscale:** High resolution version, ready for printing
- **Use as reference:** Start a new prompt using this image as inspiration
