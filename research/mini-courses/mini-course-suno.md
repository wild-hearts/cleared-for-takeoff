# Suno AI: Making Music, Even If You Can't Play

**Course summary:** A five-lesson mini-course on Suno AI for Australian adults aged 50+. Suno generates complete, original songs — with instruments, vocals, and lyrics — from a text description. Focused on the extraordinary creative possibilities: personalised birthday songs, memorial music, creative expression, and the joy of bringing musical ideas to life. Suitable for NotebookLM, video, and podcast format.

## Who This Course Is For

This course is for anyone who loves music — listening to it, feeling moved by it, having it as part of their life — but has never had the technical skill to create it.

It is for anyone who has ever had a musical idea but no way to make it real. A song for a special occasion. A tribute to someone who has passed. A piece of music in the style you love most.

No musical training required. No instruments needed. You will need words — and the willingness to try.

## Lesson 1: What Is Suno AI and Why Should You Care?

### Running time: approximately 10 minutes

Let me tell you what Suno actually does, because it is more than you probably expect.

You type a description: "A warm folk ballad about a retired schoolteacher who spent 35 years in a small country town, loved by her students, finally at peace in her garden. Female vocals, acoustic guitar, gentle and nostalgic."

Within about 30 seconds, Suno generates two complete, original songs — fully produced, with instruments, vocals, and lyrics that match your description.

Not a backing track. Not a loop. An actual song.

The quality ranges from "surprisingly good" to "genuinely remarkable." At its best, Suno produces music that sounds like it came from a real artist in a real studio. At its ordinary, it sounds like a capable indie musician. The technology has advanced further and faster than almost anyone expected.

**What does this make possible?**

Think about the occasions in life that call for specific music — music that fits the person and the moment in a way that a generic song from Spotify cannot.

A 70th birthday celebration where the music is a song written specifically about the birthday person, listing real details from their life, sung with joy. A wake where the tribute song captures something true about who this person was — their sense of humour, their love of the land, their particular style of kindness. An anniversary where the song tells the story of 40 years together.

Suno makes all of this possible in about five minutes.

It also makes personal creative expression possible in a way that has never existed before. If you have always had musical ideas — songs you could hear in your mind but never produce — Suno lets you try. Write the lyrics. Describe the style. Press create. And hear your idea given sound.

**Who makes Suno.** Suno was founded in 2022 in Cambridge, Massachusetts, by Mikey Shulman and a team of musicians and AI engineers. The company's stated mission is to make music creation accessible to everyone.

## Lesson 2: Setting It Up — Step by Step

### Running time: approximately 8 minutes

Suno is straightforward to set up and the free plan gives you 10 songs per day — more than enough to start.

### Creating an Account

1. Go to **https://www.suno.com**
2. Click "Sign up" or "Create" in the top right
3. Sign in with your Google account, Apple account, or Discord account — or create a new account with your email
4. Verify your email if prompted

### Finding Your Way Around

The Suno interface has:
- **Create:** Where you generate songs. This is your main destination.
- **Explore:** A gallery of songs created by other Suno users — a good place to hear what is possible
- **Library:** Your created songs, saved and downloadable

### Understanding Credits

On the free plan:
- You receive 50 credits per day, resetting every 24 hours
- Each song generation uses 5 credits (you generate 2 songs per creation attempt)
- You get 10 songs per day
- Free credits do not carry over — unused credits expire at midnight

### Starting to Create

1. Click "Create" in the left sidebar
2. You will see a text area. Below it, two options: "Song Description" (Suno writes everything) and "Custom" (you write the lyrics)
3. Start with "Song Description" for your first try
4. Type your description in the box
5. Click the "Create" button (it may also say "Generate")
6. Wait 20-40 seconds. Two song variations appear.
7. Press the play button to listen.

### Pro Plan

The Pro plan (USD $10/month, approximately AUD $16/month billed monthly) gives you:
- 2,500 credits per month (~500 songs)
- Commercial rights (you can use the music commercially)
- Song Editor
- Priority generation

Start with free. Subscribe to Pro if you find yourself wanting to create more songs or want to use them commercially.

This is where Suno becomes truly personal. In the Create interface, click "Custom."

## Lesson 4: The Things Nobody Tells You

### Running time: approximately 10 minutes

**Copyright — who owns what you create?**

This is the most important thing to understand about Suno, and it is clearer than you might expect.

On the free plan: your music is for personal, non-commercial use only. You can listen to it, share it with friends, play it at a private event. You cannot sell it, use it in paid videos, or monetise it anywhere.

On the Pro or Premier plan: you own the music and have full commercial rights. You can sell it, publish it on Spotify, use it in commercial videos, and monetise it on YouTube.

If you intend to use your music commercially — in a business context, in something you sell, or in a monetised online platform — subscribe to Pro first.

**The free credits reset.** This is easy to forget: if you generate 4 songs and don't use your remaining 46 credits, they are gone at midnight. Plan your creative sessions accordingly.

**Suno's vocals have quirks.** At its best, Suno's vocal quality is genuinely impressive. At its occasional worst, it can mumble consonants, stumble over difficult syllables, or phrase lyrics awkwardly when the metre is off. If this happens, try:
- Re-running the creation for different versions
- Adjusting your lyrics to have clearer, simpler syllable patterns
- Choosing a slightly different musical style

**It cannot sing specific songs.** You cannot ask Suno to recreate "Down Under" or any specific existing copyrighted song. It generates original music only. If you want something in the style of a particular song or artist, describe the style without naming the specific work.

**Some genres work better than others.** Suno is excellent at folk, country, pop, acoustic, singer-songwriter, and rock styles. It is adequate but not exceptional at jazz, classical, metal, and highly technical genres. Start with styles where it shines.

## Lesson 5: Making It Part of Your Life

### Running time: approximately 8 minutes

Suno fits naturally into three patterns of use:

**Occasion-based.** Create music for specific moments: birthdays, anniversaries, funerals, graduations, retirements, weddings. Keep a note of upcoming occasions and spend 20 minutes on a personalised song a week before. This turns into one of the most memorable things you do for the people you love.

**Creative practice.** If you enjoy the experience of creating with Suno — the pleasure of putting words to music, of exploring genres you love, of making something that did not exist before — build it into a regular practice. Set aside 30 minutes a week. Start a library of your original music. Share what you love.

**Collaborative projects.** If you are working on something larger — a family history podcast, a community event, a self-published book with an accompanying audio element — Suno can create original soundtracks, intro music, and themed pieces throughout. Original music, tailored to your content, for the cost of a Pro subscription.

**A specific suggestion for memoir and family history writers.** If you are writing a family history, consider creating a musical piece for each chapter or each generation — music in the style of the era, or music that captures something of that person's character. The intersection of written history and original music creates something genuinely powerful.

## Quick Reference Card

### Song Description Formulas

**Celebration songs:**
> "[person's name] who is [age/occasion]. They love [detail 1], [detail 2], [detail 3]. [Musical style], [vocals], [mood]."

**Tribute/memorial:**
> "A [adjective] tribute to [description of person]. They [what they loved or were known for]. [Musical style], [instruments], [mood], [not sad/gentle/moving]."

**Genre exploration:**
> "A [genre] in the style of [era or general style description]. About [subject]. [Specific instruments], [vocal style], [mood]."

**For children:**
> "A fun children's song about [topic child loves]. [Upbeat/silly/catchy], [simple language], [call-and-response if appropriate]."

---

### Custom Lyrics Template

```
[Verse 1]
Three or four lines. Introduce the subject and scene.
Specific, concrete detail works better than abstract sentiment.

[Chorus]
Two to four lines. The emotional core. Repeatable.
The part you want to stay in someone's head.

[Verse 2]
Three or four lines. A different angle or deeper detail.
Move the story forward or go deeper into feeling.

[Chorus]
Repeat.

[Bridge] (optional)
A shift in perspective or emotion before the final chorus.

[Chorus]
Final repeat.

[Outro]
One or two lines. A landing image or final thought.
```
