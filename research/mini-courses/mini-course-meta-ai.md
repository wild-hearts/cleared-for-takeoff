## Lesson 1: What Is Meta AI and Why Should You Care?

### Running time: approximately 10 minutes

In all the conversation about artificial intelligence, there is a lot of talk about what you need to set up, what new apps to download, and what passwords to create. Meta AI is different: it is already there.

Meta is the company that owns Facebook, Instagram, and WhatsApp. If you have WhatsApp on your phone — and a significant majority of Australians do — Meta AI is already installed. It is sitting in your app, waiting for you to tap it.

For anyone who has wanted to try AI but has found the idea of starting somewhere new a bit daunting — start here.

**What can it actually do?** Meta AI can answer questions, generate images, translate languages, suggest recipes, help plan events, explain things, and assist with everyday queries. It is not the most powerful AI available, but it is genuinely capable and it is right there in WhatsApp.

The most important thing to understand about Meta AI: it is designed for casual, everyday use. The questions you might ask a knowledgeable friend over the phone — that is the sweet spot. "What's a good recipe for a dinner party?" "What does this word mean?" "When is the public holiday in NSW this year?" "Can you translate this message from Italian?"

**The honest version.** Meta AI is a product of Meta, whose business model is built on advertising. When you use Meta AI, Meta may use that data as part of its advertising ecosystem. For casual, non-sensitive questions, this is probably fine. For health concerns, financial matters, or anything genuinely private, use a different tool. We will cover this honestly in Lesson 4.

**Why this is a great starting point.** The hardest part of trying something new is starting. Meta AI removes that barrier entirely. If this course gives you one thing: the confidence to tap that Meta AI button in WhatsApp and type your first question.

## Lesson 2: Setting It Up — Step by Step

### Running time: approximately 8 minutes

There is almost nothing to set up. This lesson is mostly about finding what is already there.

### Finding Meta AI in WhatsApp

**Method 1 — The dedicated chat:**
1. Open WhatsApp on your phone
2. Look at the top of your chat list. There should be a small circle icon with a sparkle or the Meta AI "M" — this is the dedicated Meta AI chat
3. Tap it. A chat window opens just like any other chat.
4. Type your question and tap the send button (the green arrow)

**Method 2 — The search bar:**
1. In WhatsApp, tap the search bar at the top of your screen (the magnifying glass)
2. Start typing a question
3. You will see an option that says "Ask Meta AI" — tap it
4. Your question is sent directly to Meta AI

**Method 3 — In a group chat:**
1. In any WhatsApp group, type @Meta AI at the start of your message
2. Follow it with your question
3. Everyone in the group sees both the question and Meta AI's answer

If you cannot find the Meta AI button, check that your WhatsApp is updated to the latest version:
- iPhone: go to the App Store, search WhatsApp, and tap "Update" if available
- Android: go to Google Play Store, search WhatsApp, tap "Update" if available

### Finding Meta AI in Facebook

1. Open the Facebook app on your phone
2. In the search bar at the top, you will see a small Meta AI icon
3. Tap it to start a conversation with Meta AI
4. Alternatively, look for "Meta AI" in the Facebook menu

### The Incognito Chat Feature

Meta AI launched "Incognito Chat" in May 2026. This gives you a private conversation that Meta cannot store or see.

1. In the Meta AI chat, tap the three dots (top right corner)
2. Select "Incognito Chat"
3. This chat is private and automatically deleted

Use this for anything you would prefer Meta not to store.

## Lesson 3: Your First Five Conversations

### Running time: approximately 15 minutes

All of these examples can be done directly in WhatsApp. Open the Meta AI chat and try them.

---

### Conversation 1: Your very first question

Start simple. Tap the Meta AI chat and type:

> **"Hello! I've never used an AI before. What kinds of things can you help me with inside WhatsApp?"**

Read the response. Meta AI will give you a friendly, clear description of what it can do. This is the best orientation you can get — directly from the tool itself.

---

### Conversation 2: A practical everyday question

Try something you might normally type into Google:

> **"I want to cook a roast lamb tonight for four people. I have a boneless leg of lamb, rosemary, garlic, and most kitchen staples. How long should I cook it and at what temperature for medium-rare? And what side dishes would you suggest?"**

You will get a clear, practical answer with times, temperatures, and suggestions. This takes 10 seconds and requires no clicking through recipes or watching an 8-minute YouTube video first.

---

### Conversation 3: An image for a group chat

Still in WhatsApp, try generating an image. Type:

> **"Imagine a cheerful birthday cake with 70 candles, decorated with Australian native flowers, white and gold colours, on a wooden table with sunlight coming through a window."**

The word "Imagine" followed by your description tells Meta AI you want an image. It generates one and sends it directly into your chat. You can then forward it to a group or send it as a birthday message.

---

### Conversation 4: Using it in a family group chat

Open a WhatsApp group you are in — perhaps a family group or a friends group. Type:

> **"@Meta AI We're all trying to plan a catch-up dinner. Can you suggest some good ways to settle on a date when everyone is busy and no one can agree?"**

Everyone in the group will see the question and Meta AI's answer. This is one of the most natural and useful ways to use Meta AI — the answer is visible to the whole group and can spark discussion.

---

### Conversation 5: Translation

If you have a message or piece of text in another language:

> **"Can you translate this from Greek (or Italian or Chinese) to English? [paste the text]"**

Or, the other way:

> **"My Italian grandchildren have sent me a voice message and I need to reply. Can you help me write: 'We are all thinking of you and can't wait to see you in December. Grandma misses you very much.' in Italian?"**

## Lesson 4: The Things Nobody Tells You

### Running time: approximately 10 minutes

**Meta's data model.** This deserves a direct, honest conversation. Meta's entire business model is built on advertising. The company analyses what you post, what you like, what you search for, and who you talk to — and uses this to show you (and others) targeted advertising. When you use Meta AI in WhatsApp and Facebook, your conversations are part of this ecosystem.

This does not mean it is wrong to use Meta AI. Many people have used Facebook and Gmail for years with full awareness that their data is being analysed for advertising. The question is whether you are making this choice consciously.

**The Incognito Chat is new and worth using.** Meta launched this feature in May 2026 and it represents a genuine improvement for privacy-conscious users. For anything you want to ask that is genuinely personal, use Incognito Chat.

**What NOT to ask Meta AI:**
- Specific health concerns (your actual diagnosis, your medications, your medical history)
- Financial account details, credit card numbers, passwords
- Personal information you would not want stored (your address combined with information about your daily schedule, for example)
- Anything that feels genuinely private

For those topics, use a dedicated AI tool like Claude, ChatGPT, or Perplexity — where you have more explicit privacy controls.

**Meta AI is less powerful than other tools.** For deep research, complex reasoning, document analysis, or nuanced writing, Meta AI is not the best option. It is designed for casual, everyday use. Know its limits.

**WhatsApp groups and privacy.** When you use @Meta AI in a group chat, everyone in the group sees the exchange. Only use this feature for questions you would be comfortable with all group members seeing.

**It may sometimes refuse.** Meta AI will decline to engage with certain topics — particularly anything related to serious harm, explicit content, or politically sensitive matters. This is standard for AI tools. It is not a flaw.

## Lesson 5: Making It Part of Your Life

### Running time: approximately 8 minutes

The easiest habit to build is the simplest: the next time you have a casual question — something you would normally Google, or something you would ask a friend if they were available — try Meta AI in WhatsApp first.

Not for anything important. Not for medical decisions or financial choices. For the ordinary, comfortable, everyday questions that come up all the time.

"Is it safe to eat leftovers that have been in the fridge for five days?" Ask Meta AI.

"What's a good gift for a 12-year-old who likes art?" Ask Meta AI.

"I've seen the word 'probate' in a document — what does it mean?" Ask Meta AI.

The value of this habit is not in any single answer. It is in building the reflex and the confidence of knowing how to use the tool. Once you are comfortable asking casual questions, the bigger questions feel less daunting.

**For family group dynamics.** Meta AI in WhatsApp groups is surprisingly fun. Use it for:
- Planning arguments ("Let's ask Meta AI what suburb has the best Italian restaurants in Melbourne")
- Trivia questions at family dinners
- Settling friendly disputes
- Shared recipe brainstorming

**A word on expectations.** Meta AI is your first step into AI, not your last one. It is approachable, convenient, and good at everyday tasks. As you get more comfortable, you will find that other tools offer more power for more demanding tasks. Think of Meta AI as the gateway, and the other tools in this series as the destinations.

## Quick Reference Card

### Meta AI in WhatsApp: Your Starter Guide

**To open Meta AI:** Tap the sparkle circle at the top of your WhatsApp chat list

**To use in a group:** Type @Meta AI before your question

**To generate an image:** Start with "Imagine" followed by what you want

**For privacy:** Tap the three dots > Incognito Chat

---

### Great Questions for Meta AI

- "Can you suggest a recipe using [ingredients I have]?"
- "What does [word or term] mean in plain English?"
- "Translate this into [language]: [text]"
- "I need to buy a gift for [person's description]. What might they like?"
- "Imagine [image description]" for an image
- "Help me write a message to [person] saying [what you want to say]"
- "What is the current [holiday/public event/sporting result]?"

---

### What Meta AI Is NOT Good For

- Complex health or medical research (use Perplexity or Claude)
- Document analysis (use ChatGPT or Claude)
- Serious financial decisions (use Perplexity with government sources)
- Deeply personal or sensitive conversations (use a dedicated tool with privacy settings)
- Generating creative writing of quality (use Claude)
