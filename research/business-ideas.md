## 2. Pricing Strategy Options

### Recommended Tiered Structure

**Free entry point:**
- One complete free lesson (Lesson 1 from the ChatGPT mini-course) — no email required
- Establishes trust, demonstrates quality, invites deeper engagement

**Individual tool mini-course:**
- Price range: AUD $39-59 per course
- One tool, five lessons, quick reference card
- Suitable as a gift; low commitment for first-time buyers

**C4TO Full Programme:**
- All 10 tool courses
- Price range: AUD $149-197 (one-time purchase)
- Most competitive value for learners who want the full picture
- Comparable to one month of a gym membership or two restaurant dinners

**Annual Companion Subscription:**
- Price range: AUD $97-147/year
- Monthly live Q&A session (video, recorded)
- Updates as tools evolve (AI changes fast)
- Access to new courses added during the year
- Community forum/discussion

**Group/Organisation Licence:**
- For U3A chapters, Probus clubs, public libraries, aged care facilities
- Price range: AUD $500-2,500/year depending on size
- Includes facilitator notes, group discussion guides, and bulk access

### Pricing Psychology Notes

- Price in whole numbers (AUD $97, not AUD $99.95) — this demographic is less responsive to retail-style pricing and more suspicious of it
- Annual billing discounts should be clear: "$97/year vs $12/month — save $47"
- Offer a 7-day refund guarantee — it removes friction and is rarely used by a genuinely engaged audience
- Bundle "give as a gift" as prominently as "buy for yourself"

## 3. Partnership Opportunities

### Tier 1: Direct Curriculum Partnerships (Highest Priority)

**U3A Australia and U3A Online**
- URL: https://www.u3aonline.org.au/
- Pitch: C4TO content as the AI literacy module across Australia's U3A network. U3A chapters typically welcome guest facilitators and curated content.
- Approach: Contact U3A Online directly with a curriculum proposal. Offer a free pilot with one chapter.
- Value exchange: U3A gets current, quality AI content for free or at cost; C4TO gets a national distribution channel and instant credibility.

**Probus Australia**
- URL: https://probusaustralia.com.au/
- Monthly club meetings need speakers. A 60-minute C4TO presentation ("Practical AI for Real Life") is exactly the format they need.
- Convert attendees to course purchasers at a Probus-member discount.
- Contact the national coordinator with a speaker proposal.

**National Seniors Australia**
- URL: https://www.nationalseniors.com.au/
- 200,000 members. Monthly magazine (Australian Seniors magazine). Member benefits programme.
- A discounted course offered to members is a clear value exchange.
- Potential for a column or article in Australian Seniors magazine (content marketing + credibility).

**Public Library Systems**
- Every state in Australia has a library network with a digital literacy mandate.
- Contact State Library digital literacy coordinators in NSW, Victoria, Queensland, SA, WA, and Tasmania.
- Offer: C4TO as the library system's AI literacy module, delivered either in-library or online.
- Libraries often have small discretionary budgets for exactly this kind of programme.

### Tier 2: Corporate/Institution Partnerships

**Superannuation Funds**
- REST Super, Hostplus, Australian Retirement Trust, Aware Super — all have member education programmes.
- AI literacy fits within member education and retirement preparation.
- Pitch: A co-branded "AI for Your Next Chapter" module offered to members approaching retirement.
- Contact: Member engagement or communications teams.

**Private Health Funds**
- Medibank, Bupa, HCF, NIB — all have member wellness programmes.
- A course on "AI for Health Navigation" (preparing for appointments, understanding test results, researching conditions) aligns with their wellness/prevention framing.
- Contact: Member wellness or digital health teams.

**Aged Care Facilities**
- Activity coordinators at residential aged care need regular programming.
- A 2-hour live workshop (AI Basics for Residents) is viable for motivated residents with devices.
- Individual facility approach is necessary — no single national pitch point.

**COTA Australia (Council on the Ageing)**
- URL: https://www.cota.org.au/
- The peak national body representing people aged 50+. Advocacy and service organisation.
- A C4TO partnership or endorsement adds significant credibility.
- Approach as a service that addresses a specific gap they have identified.

### Tier 3: Content and Media Partnerships

**ABC Australia**
- ABC's Australian Story, ABC RN, and ABC local radio all cover ageing and technology.
- A personal story angle (Naomi's own journey; stories from course participants) is the pathway in.
- ABC regional radio is particularly powerful for reaching 50+ audiences outside capital cities.

**Community Radio (3AW, 4BC, 5AA, 6PR)**
- Talk radio is heavily listened to by the 50+ demographic.
- A 10-minute chat segment ("AI Made Simple") with a real tool demonstration is strong radio content.

8. "The Best AI Tool for Australians on a Pension"
9. "Privacy and AI: What an Australian Needs to Know in 2026"
10. "AI for Your Next GP Appointment: A Practical Guide"

### YouTube/Video Content

- **Format:** Short (4-7 minute), demonstration-style, with large text on screen
- Titles that work for this audience: start with "How to..." or a specific outcome ("Get ChatGPT to write your complaint letter")
- Production value: clean background, good audio, no jump cuts — this demographic is less tolerant of fast-edit styles
- Include closed captions (accessibility and SEO)
- Series concept: "One AI Tool, One Afternoon" — each episode covers one tool in one sitting

### Podcast

- The 50+ audience over-indexes on podcast listening, particularly ABC Radio-style podcasts and spoken word content
- A C4TO podcast works as: a weekly (or fortnightly) episode exploring one practical AI use case
- Format: Naomi's voice, conversational, 15-20 minutes
- **The NotebookLM angle:** Convert the mini-course content into NotebookLM audio overviews and share them as podcast episodes. This is genuine differentiator content.
- A podcast builds discoverability in a space (older Australian learners) that has almost no dedicated audio content on AI literacy

## 5. Community Building Ideas

**Alumni Email List**
- Every course purchaser enters an alumni email programme
- Monthly: one new AI tool tip, one practical example, one "what's changed" update
- Annual subscriber renewal prompt ("AI has changed a lot this year — here's what you need to know")

**Monthly Live Q&A**
- Video call (Zoom), open to all subscribers
- Format: 15 minutes of Naomi demonstrating something new or updated, 45 minutes of questions
- Recorded and available to anyone who cannot attend live
- This is the primary value proposition of the annual subscription tier

**Private Facebook Group or Forum**
- A C4TO community where learners share what they are using AI for, ask questions, and help each other
- Moderated by Naomi (at least initially) to maintain quality and warmth
- A single rule: no judgement. Every question is a good question.

**The Buddy System**
- Pair learners who sign up around the same time to be accountability partners
- A simple check-in: "Did you try X this week? What happened?"
- Older adults who learn together are much more likely to complete courses and integrate new habits

**Annual "What I Did With AI" Showcase**
- A yearly email/post inviting learners to share one thing AI helped them do this year
- The responses become testimonials, stories, and content
- The accumulated stories of what 60-70-year-old Australians are doing with AI is genuinely remarkable content

## 8. Social Media Angles That Actually Work for 50+ Australians

**What does not work:**
- Hype and urgency ("Don't get left behind!")
- Tech-bro framing ("AI is going to change everything")
- Condescension ("Even your grandma can do this!")
- Jargon without explanation

**What does work:**

- **Real stories with real names (permission granted).** "Geoff, 71, used Claude to write his father's eulogy when he couldn't find the words. He said it was the most important thing he'd done with a computer."
- **Practical demonstrations.** A 60-second video of a real task being completed. No commentary beyond "here's how to do it."
- **"I tried this and here's what actually happened"** — demystifying the real experience removes the fear of the unknown.
- **Myth-busting.** "AI will steal your personal information if you use it" — true or false? A post that addresses real fears with real answers builds trust.
- **The intergenerational angle.** "How to talk to your grandchildren about what they're doing with AI at school" — this framing resonates deeply.

**Platforms that matter for this audience:**
- Facebook (still dominant in the 50+ demographic)
- Facebook Groups (local community groups are often the highest-engagement spaces)
- LinkedIn (for retired professionals who still identify with their working identity)
- Podcast directories (Apple Podcasts, Spotify)

**Platforms to avoid for now:**
- TikTok (not the primary platform for 55+ Australians)
- X/Twitter (declining relevance for this demographic)
- Instagram Reels (can work but requires more video production investment)

## 9. First 90 Days of Launch

### Days 1-30: Foundation

**Week 1-2: Soft launch to existing network**
- Send personal email to 50-100 people in Naomi's network: friends, family, former colleagues, professional contacts
- Ask for feedback, not sales: "I've built something I'd love you to try and tell me what you think"
- Identify two or three early adopters who will become testimonials and case studies

**Week 3-4: Free content launch**
- Publish the first free lesson (ChatGPT Lesson 1) publicly — no email required
- Write and publish first blog post ("Is ChatGPT Safe? An Honest Answer for Australians")
- Launch a simple social media presence — Facebook page and one Facebook community group

### Days 31-60: Building Visibility

**Week 5-6: Partnership outreach begins**
- Contact U3A Online with curriculum partnership proposal
- Identify 3-5 Probus clubs in major cities; contact speakers committee with presentation proposal
- Contact one public library system (start local — the library closest to you)

**Week 7-8: Community seeding**
- Launch private Facebook group for early learners
- Post three times per week — one practical tip, one question for community response, one behind-the-scenes look at the course
- Ask the 3-5 early adopters to share their experience in the group

### Days 61-90: Momentum

**Week 9-10: First paid promotion**
- Run a small Facebook advertising test. Budget: AUD $500 for the first campaign.
- Target: Women aged 55-70, Australia, interests in "learning," "technology," "retirement planning," "grandchildren"
- Offer: The free first lesson as the ad's call to action (lower friction than asking for a purchase)
- Track: Cost per click, free lesson completion rate, conversion to paid course

**Week 11-12: First live event**
- Run a free 60-minute Zoom session: "AI Basics for Australians — A Live Introduction"
- Promote via the email list, Facebook group, and any Probus/U3A connections made in weeks 5-8
- Record it. Repurpose as content.

**Week 13: Review and recalibrate**
- What worked? What did not?
- What questions keep coming up that are not in the current content?
- Where are people getting stuck? (This informs the next course to develop)
- What does the data say about which tools the audience is most interested in?

## 10. The Numbers to Watch

Beyond course purchases, these are the metrics that indicate genuine health:

- **Email open rate:** Target 30%+ (excellent for this audience who care about what they signed up for)
- **Course completion rate:** Target 60%+ (most online courses are below 15%; this audience, if engaged, completes)
- **Referral rate:** How many new customers come from existing customer recommendations? Target 30%+
- **Repeat purchase rate:** Do people who buy one mini-course buy another? Target 40%+
- **Live session attendance:** Of monthly subscribers, what percentage attend the live Q&A? Even 20% is good; 40% is exceptional
