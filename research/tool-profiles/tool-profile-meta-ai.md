# Tool Profile: Meta AI (WhatsApp and Facebook)

**Document summary:** A comprehensive profile of Meta AI for Australian adults aged 50+. This is the AI assistant built into WhatsApp and Facebook — apps many people in this demographic already use daily. Covers features, pricing, privacy considerations, and practical use cases.

**Last updated:** May 2026

---

## What Is Meta AI and Who Makes It?

Meta AI is an AI assistant made by Meta, the company that owns Facebook, Instagram, and WhatsApp. Mark Zuckerberg founded Facebook in 2004; the company rebranded to Meta in 2021 to reflect its expansion beyond social media into AI and what it called "the metaverse."

Meta AI is built directly into WhatsApp and Facebook. If you use WhatsApp — and a majority of Australians do — you have probably already seen it. It appears as a small circle icon in your WhatsApp chat list, or as a search feature when you try to find something on Facebook.

The AI is powered by Meta's own large language model called Llama. As of 2026, the version used in WhatsApp and Facebook is Llama 4.

The key distinction between Meta AI and the other tools in this series: you do not need to download anything new or create a new account. It is already on your phone, inside apps you already use.

## Free vs Paid Tiers (Pricing in AUD, May 2026)

### Meta AI (Free — built into WhatsApp and Facebook)
- Available to all WhatsApp and Facebook users at no additional charge
- Text-based conversations
- Image generation ("Imagine..." command)
- Web search integration
- Access to general knowledge and question-answering

**Cost:** $0 (included with your existing WhatsApp/Facebook account)

### Meta One Plus (Testing phase as of May 2026)
- Higher-capacity queries
- More advanced reasoning for complex tasks
- Additional image and video generation capabilities across Meta apps

**Cost:** Approximately USD $7.99/month (AUD ~$12.50/month). This plan was in early testing as of May 2026; availability may vary.

### Meta One Premium (Testing phase as of May 2026)
- Full advanced AI capabilities
- Priority access to frontier models
- Enhanced video and image generation

**Cost:** Approximately USD $19.99/month (AUD ~$31/month). Also in early testing phase.

*Note: Meta subscriptions are billed through the App Store (iPhone) or Google Play Store (Android), which means Australian GST applies.*

### WhatsApp Plus (Separate from AI features)
- Profile customisation, app themes, additional pinned chats
- Not related to AI capability

**Cost:** USD $2.99/month

## What It Is Genuinely Good At

**Zero new account required.** For someone who finds creating new accounts and remembering passwords daunting, the fact that Meta AI lives inside WhatsApp is significant. If you already use WhatsApp, you are one tap away from using AI for the first time.

**Conversational and casual.** Meta AI is designed for everyday conversation. It handles casual questions, quick lookups, recipe ideas, and simple explanations well. The tone is relaxed and friendly — less formal than some other tools.

**Quick, practical answers.** "What's a good recipe for a slow cooker lamb stew using ingredients I already have?" "What time does Aldi close today?" "What does this term mean in my lease?" Meta AI handles these comfortably.

**Image generation in WhatsApp.** Type "Imagine a golden retriever playing in autumn leaves" and Meta AI generates a photo-quality image directly in your WhatsApp chat. You can share it immediately. Surprisingly useful for birthday cards, social media posts, and fun.

**Group chat assistance.** In WhatsApp group chats, you can tag @Meta AI to ask a question. The group gets the answer. This is useful for shared travel planning, event organising, and family trivia debates.

**Language and translation.** "Translate this message to Italian" works reliably in WhatsApp. Useful for communicating with overseas family, friends, or while travelling.

**Web search.** Meta AI searches the web for current information when needed. It is not as rigorous about citing sources as Perplexity, but it provides current information for everyday queries.

## What It Struggles With

**Less powerful than standalone AI tools.** For deep research, complex reasoning, document analysis, or nuanced writing, Meta AI is not the most capable option. ChatGPT, Claude, and Perplexity are better for demanding tasks.

**Privacy concerns are significant.** Meta's business model is built on data. Your conversations with Meta AI contribute to Meta's understanding of you and your interests. This is a genuine concern, particularly given Meta's track record on privacy. (See the Privacy section below for detail.)

**Incognito mode (just launched).** Meta launched "Incognito Chat" with Meta AI in WhatsApp in May 2026, where conversations are processed privately and disappear by default. This is a positive development, but it is a new feature and worth monitoring.

**Not suitable for sensitive topics.** Discussing health conditions, financial decisions, family difficulties, or legal matters with an AI inside Facebook/WhatsApp means Meta potentially has access to this information for advertising purposes. Use other tools for sensitive queries.

**No document upload.** As of mid-2026, Meta AI in WhatsApp does not support uploading documents like PDFs for analysis. For document-based tasks, use ChatGPT, Claude, or Gemini.

**Inconsistency.** The quality of Meta AI's responses can vary. It is better at simple tasks than complex ones.

## Best Use Cases for Someone Aged 50+

1. **Your very first AI experience.** If the thought of downloading new apps, creating accounts, and figuring out a new interface feels too hard — start here. Open WhatsApp, tap the Meta AI circle at the top of your chat list, and type "Hello. What can you help me with?" That is all.

2. **Quick everyday questions while in conversation.** You are chatting with your family on WhatsApp and someone asks what year something happened, or what the population of a city is, or what the rules of a game are. Tag @Meta AI in the group — instant answer.

3. **Recipe ideas.** "I have chicken thighs, sweet potato, and spinach in the fridge. What can I make for dinner tonight that takes less than 45 minutes?" It will give you a recipe with instructions.

4. **Fun image generation.** Create a custom image for a grandchild's birthday message, a funny image for a WhatsApp group, or a personalised greeting card image — directly inside WhatsApp.

5. **Simple translation.** Paste text in a foreign language and ask for a translation. Fast and usually accurate for common European and Asian languages.

6. **Casual research.** "Is it safe to eat leftover rice that's been in the fridge for four days?" "What's the difference between a fixed and variable rate mortgage?" Quick, conversational answers for the kind of questions you would look up on Google.

## How to Get Started: Step by Step

**In WhatsApp:**
1. Open WhatsApp on your phone
2. At the top of your chat list, look for a circle with a sparkle icon — this is Meta AI
3. Tap it. A chat window opens.
4. Type your question. Press send (the green arrow)
5. Meta AI responds, usually within a few seconds.

**Alternatively:**
1. Tap the search bar at the top of WhatsApp
2. Start typing a question — Meta AI will offer to search or answer
3. Tap "Ask Meta AI" to direct your question to the AI

**In a group chat:**
1. Type "@Meta AI" in any group message
2. Follow with your question
3. Everyone in the group sees the answer

**For image generation:**
1. Type "Imagine [description of image you want]"
2. Meta AI generates and displays the image
3. Tap and hold to save it to your photos

**Privacy mode (Incognito Chat — new feature):**
1. In your Meta AI chat, tap the three dots (top right)
2. Select "Incognito Chat"
3. Conversations in this mode are processed privately and deleted automatically

## Privacy and Data Considerations (Australian Law)

**Meta's data model.** Meta's business model is advertising. The company analyses data about you — what you post, what you search, who you talk to, what you like — to show you targeted advertisements. When you use Meta AI, your conversations are part of this data ecosystem. Be clear-eyed about this.

**Official privacy statement from Meta:** Meta says AI conversations may be used to improve its AI models and services. The Incognito Chat feature (launched May 2026) provides a protected environment where conversations are not stored and cannot be seen by Meta.

**What the OAIC says:** Australia's OAIC guidance notes that using AI services provided by global platforms means your data is governed primarily by those platforms' terms of service, not the Australian Privacy Act, unless you are an Australian entity using the platform commercially.

**Practical guidance:**
- Use Incognito Chat for anything sensitive
- Do not discuss health conditions, financial details, family disputes, or personal crises in Meta AI's standard chat mode
- Do not enter personal identification information (Medicare number, TFN, bank details) into any Meta product
- Remember: if you use Facebook and Instagram on the same device, Meta connects the dots between all of them

**The honest framing.** Meta AI is convenient because it is free and already on your phone. This convenience is partly the product of Meta having access to your data. If that tradeoff concerns you, use one of the other tools in this series instead. If you are comfortable with it, Meta AI is genuinely useful for everyday, non-sensitive tasks.

## Honest Assessment: Is It Worth It?

The question is slightly different here because "it" is free and already on your phone. The real question is whether to use it.

For casual, everyday, non-sensitive use — recipes, quick questions, fun image generation, group chat assistance — yes, Meta AI is practical and accessible. The zero-friction setup is a genuine advantage for someone making their first steps with AI.

For research, document analysis, writing assistance, or anything involving personal health or financial information — use a dedicated tool (ChatGPT, Claude, Perplexity) instead. The privacy considerations are meaningful, and those tools are more capable for demanding tasks.

**Verdict:** An excellent first AI experience for WhatsApp users who want to try AI with no setup required. Not the right tool for serious research or sensitive personal matters. Think of it as the casual conversation option — useful, available, but not where you go with your most important questions.
