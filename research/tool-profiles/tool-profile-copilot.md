# Tool Profile: Microsoft Copilot

**Document summary:** A comprehensive profile of Microsoft Copilot for Australian adults aged 50+. Covers what it is, free and paid pricing, strengths, limitations, privacy considerations, and five practical prompts.

**Last updated:** May 2026

---

## What Is Microsoft Copilot and Who Makes It?

Microsoft Copilot is Microsoft's AI assistant. Microsoft is one of the world's oldest and largest technology companies — the maker of Windows, Office (now Microsoft 365), and Xbox. In 2023 and 2024, Microsoft invested billions of dollars into OpenAI (the company that makes ChatGPT) and used that partnership to build Copilot, which runs on many of the same models that power ChatGPT.

This is an important detail: Copilot and ChatGPT are closely related. For many tasks, they produce similar results. The difference is where Copilot lives — it is built into Windows, Microsoft Edge, Bing Search, Word, Excel, Outlook, PowerPoint, and Teams. If you use any Microsoft product, Copilot is likely already on your screen.

There are two distinct versions of Copilot:

**Microsoft Copilot (free/personal):** The standalone AI assistant available at copilot.microsoft.com or built into Windows 11. This is aimed at individuals.

**Microsoft 365 Copilot:** The workplace version, integrated into Word, Excel, Outlook, PowerPoint, and Teams. This is aimed at businesses and requires an existing Microsoft 365 subscription plus an additional monthly fee.

For most people aged 50+ using Microsoft products at home, the free personal Copilot is the relevant one.

## Free vs Paid Tiers (Pricing in AUD, May 2026)

### Copilot (Free Personal)
- Access via copilot.microsoft.com, the Windows 11 Start menu, or Microsoft Edge
- Powered by GPT-4o (same model as ChatGPT free tier)
- Web search integrated (draws on Bing)
- Image generation via Designer (DALL-E based)
- Voice mode
- Daily conversation limit
- Works without a Microsoft account (anonymous), though signing in unlocks history

**Cost:** $0

### Copilot Pro (Personal)
- Priority access to latest AI models
- Faster image generation
- Copilot integration in Microsoft 365 Personal and Family (Word, Excel, Outlook, PowerPoint, OneNote)
- Higher daily limits

**Cost:** Approximately AUD $30-36 per user per month

### Microsoft 365 Copilot (Business)
- Full integration across all Microsoft 365 apps
- AI in Outlook (summarise emails, draft replies), Word (draft from scratch, summarise documents), Excel (analyse data with natural language), PowerPoint (create presentations from documents), Teams (meeting summaries)
- Requires existing Microsoft 365 Business subscription

**Cost:** AUD $33 per user per month (promotional pricing through June 2026; standard pricing higher thereafter)

## What It Is Genuinely Good At

**Familiarity.** If you have been using Microsoft products for years — and many Australians aged 50+ have — Copilot feels less intimidating than a new standalone app. It is built into software you already know.

**Edge browser integration.** Microsoft Edge has Copilot built into the right-hand panel. You can have a web page open on the left and ask Copilot questions about it on the right. This is particularly useful for reading news articles ("Summarise this article for me"), understanding product pages ("What are the reviews saying about this dishwasher?"), or comparing information across tabs.

**Word and Outlook integration (with Pro).** With Copilot Pro, the Word toolbar gets an AI button. Highlight a paragraph and ask Copilot to improve it. Ask it to summarise your document. In Outlook, ask it to draft a reply to an email, or summarise a long email thread so you can catch up quickly.

**Web search currency.** Copilot uses Bing for web searches, meaning it has access to current information. It is good at tasks requiring up-to-date information: "What are the current requirements for an Australian to visit Japan?" or "What is the current RBA cash rate?"

**Image generation.** The Designer (DALL-E based) image generation is good and is included in the free tier, which ChatGPT does not offer at the same level.

**Privacy framing.** Microsoft has been more explicit than some competitors about enterprise privacy: the Microsoft 365 Copilot commercial data protection policy means your conversations are not used to train AI models if you are a Microsoft 365 commercial subscriber.

## What It Struggles With

**The naming confusion is real.** "Copilot" appears in Windows, in Edge, in Teams, in Office, and as a standalone product. Each one works slightly differently. This inconsistency confuses even technically literate users. For someone new to AI, it can be genuinely bewildering to discover that the Copilot in Word behaves differently from the Copilot in Edge.

**Writing quality.** As a direct product of the same models as ChatGPT, Copilot produces similar writing. But in practice, its outputs sometimes feel slightly more corporate and less natural than Claude's. For polished personal writing, Claude may be preferable.

**Free tier limitations.** The free Copilot has more restrictions than the free tiers of ChatGPT or Gemini. You can hit limits more quickly.

**Microsoft account requirement for best experience.** Without a Microsoft account, you get a reduced experience. Creating and managing a Microsoft account is an added friction point for some users.

**Excel and PowerPoint AI.** The Office integration sounds compelling but requires Copilot Pro (paid) and works best in more complex documents. For basic spreadsheets and simple presentations, the investment may not be justified.

## Best Use Cases for Someone Aged 50+

1. **Catching up on a long email thread.** In Outlook with Copilot Pro: a button appears at the top of any long email thread saying "Summarise this thread." One click gives you a three-paragraph summary of what has been discussed. Genuinely useful when you come back from holidays to 400 emails.

2. **Getting answers while browsing.** With Edge browser: you are reading an article about the new aged care reforms and you want to understand what a particular term means, or you want to check whether what you are reading is consistent with other sources. Open Copilot in the sidebar without leaving the page.

3. **Drafting a document in Word.** Open a blank Word document, click the Copilot icon, and describe what you want: "A formal letter to my council requesting a review of my rates assessment, noting the assessment seems high compared to recent sales in my street." Copilot drafts it; you edit.

4. **Image generation for personal use.** "Create a watercolour-style image of a verandah overlooking the Australian bush at dusk, with a dog sleeping on the steps." This generates something you can use for a card, a social media post, or just to enjoy.

5. **Research on specific topics.** "What are the current Australian Pension eligibility requirements for someone turning 67 this year, and what assets and income tests apply?" Copilot will search and provide a current, cited response.

6. **Understanding Windows itself.** "How do I stop Windows 11 from automatically installing updates at inconvenient times?" Copilot, built into Windows, can answer questions about your own computer and walk you through technical steps.

## How to Get Started: Step by Step

**Option 1 — Via the web (no installation needed):**
1. Open any browser and go to **https://copilot.microsoft.com**
2. You can use it without an account, or sign in with your Microsoft account (if you have Outlook.com or Hotmail, you already have one)
3. Type a question in the chat box at the bottom and press Enter

**Option 2 — Via Windows 11:**
1. Look for the Copilot icon in your taskbar (it looks like a small colourful circle)
2. Click it; the Copilot panel opens on the right side of your screen
3. Type a question

**Option 3 — Via Microsoft Edge browser:**
1. Open Edge
2. Look for the Copilot icon in the top-right corner of the browser (looks like a small circle)
3. Click it to open the Copilot sidebar alongside whatever web page you are viewing

**Setting up Copilot Pro:**
1. Go to https://copilot.microsoft.com
2. Click on your account icon (top right)
3. Select "Get Copilot Pro"
4. Follow the billing steps — requires a Microsoft account

## Five Specific Prompts for This Audience

**1. Summarising news:**
> "I want to understand what's happening with the current housing affordability debate in Australia. Can you search for recent articles and give me a balanced summary of the main arguments — what the government is proposing, what critics say, and what's actually likely to change in the next two years?"

**2. Help with Word documents:**
> "I need to write a cover letter for a volunteer coordinator position at my local community centre. I'm 64, recently retired from 30 years as a secondary school teacher, and I want to emphasise my organisational skills and experience working with people of all ages. Can you draft a letter of about 300 words that's warm and professional?"

**3. Technical help with Windows:**
> "My computer is running slowly lately. It's a Windows 11 laptop that's about three years old. Can you give me a step-by-step checklist of things I can try to speed it up without losing any of my files or settings?"

**4. Image generation:**
> "Can you create an image for my granddaughter's birthday invitation? She is turning 8, loves horses and the colour purple, and we're having the party at a park. I'd like something cheerful and cartoon-style."

**5. Research and comparison:**
> "I'm comparing three private health insurance extras covers for a couple aged 65+. Can you help me think through the key questions I should be asking when comparing them — what are the things insurance companies don't advertise clearly that actually matter when you get older?"

## Privacy and Data Considerations (Australian Law)

**Microsoft's privacy approach.** Microsoft has made explicit commitments about data protection for commercial Copilot users: your data is not used to train AI models when you use Microsoft 365 Copilot with a commercial subscription. For personal (free) use, the standard Microsoft privacy policy applies, which does allow Microsoft to use data for service improvement.

**Edge Browsing History.** If Copilot in Edge can see your browsing activity, consider what this means — your health research, financial browsing, and personal interests are potentially visible to the AI. You can control this in Edge Settings > Privacy, search and services.

**Microsoft account data.** Your Microsoft account ties together OneDrive, Outlook, Teams, Xbox, and more. Granting Copilot access to these services means considerable data integration. This is a feature for convenience and a concern for privacy; the choice is yours.

**Windows data collection.** Windows 11 collects diagnostic data by default. This is separate from but related to Copilot — Microsoft has access to your device activity. Privacy settings can be adjusted in Settings > Privacy and security.

**The Recall feature (important note):** Microsoft introduced a feature called "Recall" in 2025 that takes regular screenshots of everything you do on your PC and makes it searchable via AI. This feature attracted significant privacy criticism. It can be disabled in Settings > Privacy and security > Recall and snapshots. Many security experts recommend disabling it.

## Honest Assessment: Is It Worth It?

For anyone using a Windows PC or Microsoft 365, the free Copilot is worth using simply because it is already there. The Edge integration in particular adds genuine value when browsing.

Copilot Pro (AUD $30-36/month) is worth considering if you write frequently in Word or manage email in Outlook. The document drafting and email summarisation features are practical, not gimmicky.

The main consideration is familiarity. If you are already comfortable in the Microsoft ecosystem and find the idea of a new app intimidating, Copilot is the lowest-friction path into AI. It is not always the most powerful option, but it is the most familiar.

For Mac users with no Microsoft investment, the other tools in this series (ChatGPT, Claude, Gemini) will serve better.

**Verdict:** Best for Windows/Microsoft 365 users who want AI integrated into software they already use. Excellent free tier for general browsing and questions. Pro subscription adds meaningful value for heavy Word and Outlook users.
