# Tool Profile: Claude (Anthropic)

**Document summary:** A comprehensive profile of Claude, the AI assistant made by Anthropic, for Australian adults aged 50+. Covers capabilities, pricing in AUD, strengths, limitations, privacy considerations, practical use cases, and five specific prompts.

**Last updated:** May 2026

## What Is Claude and Who Makes It?

Claude is an AI assistant made by Anthropic, a US-based AI safety company founded in 2021. The name Claude comes from Claude Shannon, the mathematician who laid the foundations for information theory. Anthropic was started by former members of OpenAI who wanted to build AI with a stronger focus on safety and reliability.

In 2026, Claude is widely regarded as one of the most capable and thoughtful AI assistants available. It is particularly well-regarded for writing quality, careful reasoning, nuanced responses, and willingness to say "I'm not sure" rather than confidently providing a wrong answer. This last quality matters more than people realise.

You access Claude at **claude.ai** — through a web browser or mobile app. There is no need to download anything to use the web version.

Claude is available in several versions. The one most people use is Claude Sonnet, which offers an excellent balance of capability and speed. Claude Opus (the most powerful version) is available on paid plans for tasks requiring deep analysis or extended reasoning.

## Free vs Paid Tiers (Pricing in AUD, May 2026)

### Free Plan
- Access to Claude Sonnet
- Limited daily conversations (the exact limit varies; you may hit it after several substantial conversations)
- Basic web search
- Basic file uploads
- The Artifacts feature (Claude builds things — documents, tables, plans — in a separate panel)
- Chat memory (basic)

**Cost:** $0

### Claude Pro
- Higher message limits (5x more than free)
- Access to Claude Opus (the most powerful model)
- Priority access during busy periods
- Extended research mode (Claude autonomously browses, reads multiple sources, and synthesises)
- Full Microsoft 365 integration

**Cost:** Approximately AUD $31 per month (including GST). Billed in Australian dollars.

*Note: Anthropic charges Australian GST on subscriptions. The USD base price is $20/month; AUD pricing includes the exchange rate and GST component.*

### Claude Team (for organisations)
- Everything in Pro, shared workspaces, admin controls
- **Cost:** Approximately AUD $45 per user per month

## What It Is Genuinely Good At

**Writing quality.** Claude is widely considered the best AI assistant for producing high-quality, natural-sounding prose. Its writing has fewer clichés, fewer awkward phrases, and better structure than most competitors. If you care about the quality of what comes out, Claude is often the right choice.

**Long documents.** Claude can hold a very long context window — it can read an entire book, a full legal contract, or a lengthy government document in one go, and then answer questions about it. This is practically useful for anyone dealing with detailed insurance policies, financial product disclosure statements, or medical documentation.

**Honest uncertainty.** Claude is more likely than other AI tools to say "I'm not confident about this" or "I'd recommend verifying this with a professional." This is a feature, not a bug. It means you can trust its confident answers more.

**Complex reasoning.** If you give Claude a genuinely complicated problem — a multi-step planning task, an ethical question, a document requiring careful analysis — it tends to work through it methodically.

**Explaining things.** Claude gives excellent explanations of complex topics. It adjusts its level of detail and vocabulary to match what you need.

**Creative writing.** Fiction, poetry, speeches, eulogies, family histories — Claude produces creative work with genuine craft. It responds well to feedback and iterates.

**Research mode.** With Claude Pro, research mode lets you give Claude a complex question and it will autonomously browse multiple sources, read and compare them, and give you a synthesised, cited response. This is particularly useful for health research, product comparisons, or understanding a complex issue.

## What It Struggles With

**Image generation:** Claude does not generate images. It can analyse and describe images you upload, but it cannot create them. For image generation, ChatGPT (with DALL-E) or Midjourney are the right tools.

**Voice mode:** Claude does not have a voice conversation mode equivalent to ChatGPT's Advanced Voice Mode. You can use text-to-speech features in your browser or device to have messages read aloud, but it is not designed for hands-free conversation.

**Very recent news:** Claude has a knowledge cutoff and while it has web search capability, it is not primarily designed for real-time news or very recent events.

**Maths and calculations:** Like all language-model-based AI, Claude can make arithmetic errors. For anything requiring precise calculations, verify independently.

**Australian specifics:** Claude is knowledgeable but was trained predominantly on English-language content that skews US and UK. It knows less about Australian-specific systems, laws, and services than ideal. Always check local applicability.

## Best Use Cases for Someone Aged 50+

1. **Writing that matters.** Any document where quality of expression counts: letters to government departments, complaints, appeals, tribute speeches, submissions to councils or committees. Claude produces well-crafted writing that does not sound generated.

2. **Reading and understanding long documents.** Estate planning documents, strata bylaws, aged care funding instruments, financial adviser reports, insurance product disclosure statements. Paste the document or upload the PDF and ask specific questions.

3. **Family history and memoir.** Claude is an exceptional writing partner for family history projects. Share your notes or memories, ask it to help structure a chapter, then work through it together. It asks good questions.

4. **Health research.** "I've been diagnosed with atrial fibrillation. Can you explain what this means, what the standard treatment options are, and what questions are most important to ask my cardiologist?" It will give you thorough, considered information — and it will remind you to verify with your doctor.

5. **Navigating complex bureaucracy.** Centrelink, Medicare, the NDIS, My Aged Care — Australian government systems can be bewildering. Claude can help you understand what you are entitled to, what forms you need, and how a process works.

6. **Learning and intellectual engagement.** Claude handles sophisticated conversation. If you want to discuss books, debate ideas, explore philosophy, understand history, or simply think through a complex problem with a knowledgeable interlocutor — this is its natural habitat.

7. **Business and contract review.** Tradie quotes, small business contracts, lease agreements — upload the document and ask Claude to flag anything unusual or concerning.

## How to Get Started: Step by Step

1. Open your browser and go to **https://claude.ai**
2. Click "Sign up" — you can sign in with your Google or Apple account, or create a new account with your email
3. Confirm your email if prompted
4. You will see a clean, simple interface with a text box. This is deliberately less cluttered than ChatGPT.
5. Type your first message in the text box and press Enter
6. A good first prompt: **"Hello Claude. I'm new to AI assistants. Can you give me a sense of what you're particularly good at and how I might get the most out of you?"**

**Uploading a document:**
- Click the paperclip icon next to the text box
- Select a file from your computer (PDF, Word doc, or text file)
- Add your question in the text box: "Can you summarise the key points of this document?"
- Press Enter

**Using the Artifacts feature:**
When Claude creates a document, spreadsheet, or structured output, it appears in a separate panel on the right side of the screen. You can copy it, download it, or ask Claude to modify it.

## Five Specific Prompts for This Audience

**1. Understanding an aged care document:**
> "I've just received a letter from My Aged Care about my mother's Home Support assessment. I'm finding it confusing. [Paste document text here] Can you explain in plain English: (a) what level of support she has been assessed for, (b) what this means practically, (c) what our next steps should be, and (d) what questions we should ask when we call them?"

**2. Writing a tribute or eulogy:**
> "I've been asked to say a few words at my brother-in-law's funeral next week. He was 74, a retired builder, loved AFL (he barracked for Geelong), had four grandchildren he adored, and was known for his terrible puns and for always turning up when someone needed help. I'm not a confident public speaker — I'd like something warm, brief (around three minutes), and that captures who he was without being too formal. Can you help me write it?"

**3. Complex health research:**
> "My GP has recommended I consider a hip replacement in the next year or two. I'm 69, otherwise healthy, active, and a bit nervous about surgery. Can you give me a thorough overview of: what the operation involves, typical recovery timeline, realistic outcomes for someone my age, what questions to ask my orthopaedic surgeon at the first appointment, and any alternatives worth discussing?"

**4. Understanding your financial documents:**
> "I'm looking at the annual statement from my superannuation fund. [Paste relevant sections] Can you help me understand: (a) whether my fund has performed well compared to typical benchmarks, (b) what fees I'm paying and whether they seem reasonable, (c) what my projected retirement balance might be if I continue as is, and (d) whether there's anything here I should discuss with a financial adviser?"

**5. Planning a family project:**
> "I'd like to create a family history book to give to my children and grandchildren as a legacy project. I have notes, some photographs I can describe, and a general sense of the stories I want to tell. Can you help me: (a) structure the project in a realistic way, (b) suggest what sections to include, (c) give me a template for interviewing older relatives while I still can, and (d) help me think through what format the final book might take?"

## Privacy and Data Considerations (Australian Law)

**What Anthropic does with your data:** By default, Anthropic may use conversations to improve Claude, but they have a clearer opt-out process than some competitors. You can opt out at any time in Settings > Privacy.

**Claude's specific approach to sensitive content:** Claude is designed to handle sensitive topics carefully. In health or legal contexts, it will typically remind you to consult a professional. This is not a limitation — it is responsible design.

**What not to share:** The same rules apply as with all AI tools. Do not enter your TFN, Medicare number, bank account details, passwords, or any information you would not want stored on a US company's servers.

**Australian Privacy Act implications:** Anthropic is a US company; Australian individuals using Claude personally are primarily governed by Anthropic's terms of service, not the Australian Privacy Act (which applies to organisations). However, if your organisation uses Claude to process personal information about Australians, the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) applies. The OAIC has published guidance on this.

**Data retention:** Conversation history is retained in your account until you delete it. You can delete individual conversations or your entire history at any time from the Settings menu.

## Honest Assessment: Is It Worth It?

Claude's free tier is genuinely capable and the interface is clean and less overwhelming than some competitors. It is a particularly good choice as a first AI tool for someone who values clear, high-quality writing and thoughtful responses.

The Pro plan (AUD $31/month) adds meaningful capability: more messages, access to the more powerful Opus model for complex tasks, and the research feature that autonomously browses and synthesises sources. For someone using Claude regularly for health research, document analysis, or serious writing projects, the Pro plan pays for itself in saved time and improved output quality.

For beginners, start with the free plan. Claude's clean interface and honest responses make it one of the most beginner-friendly AI tools available.

**Verdict:** Excellent for writing, reading long documents, and thoughtful conversation. The best choice for quality-conscious users. Free plan is a strong starting point.
