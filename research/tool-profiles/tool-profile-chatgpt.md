## What Is ChatGPT and Who Makes It?

ChatGPT is an AI assistant made by OpenAI, a US-based company founded in 2015. It is the most widely used AI chatbot in the world, with hundreds of millions of users across more than 180 countries.

You talk to ChatGPT by typing questions or requests into a text box, and it writes back — immediately, in plain English, in whatever length is appropriate. You can also speak to it using Advanced Voice Mode, where it listens to your voice and responds aloud, like a phone call with a very knowledgeable assistant.

ChatGPT is not a search engine. It does not look up a list of websites and show you links (though it can now browse the web when asked). Instead, it draws on an enormous base of knowledge from books, articles, websites, and documents it was trained on, and it generates a thoughtful response each time.

As of 2026, ChatGPT can:
- Hold a conversation, remember what you said earlier in the session, and build on it
- Browse the internet for current information
- Generate and edit images (with the paid plan)
- Analyse documents you upload — PDFs, Word files, spreadsheets
- Speak and listen in real time using voice mode
- Write, edit, summarise, explain, translate, and draft

## Free vs Paid Tiers (Pricing in AUD, May 2026)

### Free Plan
- Access to GPT-4o (the current standard model)
- Text conversations with a daily message limit
- Basic web browsing
- Basic image generation (limited)
- Voice mode (limited)
- No memory across sessions (each conversation starts fresh)

**Cost:** $0

### ChatGPT Plus
- Unlimited access to GPT-4o and newer reasoning models
- Full image generation with DALL-E 3
- Advanced Voice Mode (real-time spoken conversation)
- Memory across conversations (it learns your preferences over time)
- File uploads and document analysis
- Access to newer models as they release

**Cost:** Approximately AUD $35 per month (including GST). Billed in Australian dollars. Prices may vary slightly with exchange rate fluctuations.

### ChatGPT Team (for groups or small businesses)
- Everything in Plus, plus shared workspace and admin tools
- **Cost:** Approximately AUD $30-36 per user per month (minimum two users)

## What It Is Genuinely Good At

**Writing assistance:** ChatGPT is exceptionally good at drafting emails, letters, and messages. Give it the key points you want to make, the tone you want, and the length, and it produces a professional draft in seconds. It is equally good at editing something you have already written — you paste your text, say "make this clearer" or "make this less formal," and it does.

**Explanation and learning:** Ask it to explain anything — how a medication works, what a financial term means, how to understand your electricity bill, what the difference between a will and a power of attorney is — and it gives you a clear, plain-English answer. You can follow up: "Can you explain that more simply?" or "Give me an example."

**Research and summarising:** Upload a long PDF (an insurance policy, a medical report, a council document) and ask it to summarise the key points. Or ask it to search the web and summarise what it finds on a topic.

**Planning and organising:** "Help me plan a two-week road trip through South Australia in October" produces a genuinely useful itinerary. "Help me write a grocery list for a dinner party for eight" works too.

**Creative projects:** Writing a poem for a grandchild's birthday, drafting a speech, writing a short story, composing a cover letter — ChatGPT handles all of these.

**Voice conversations:** This is underrated. Advanced Voice Mode lets you have a genuine back-and-forth spoken conversation. If you find typing difficult, or if you think better out loud, this changes everything.

## What It Struggles With

**Accuracy on specific facts:** ChatGPT can get facts wrong — particularly dates, names, statistics, and medical specifics. This is called "hallucination" in the industry. The model generates plausible-sounding text, but plausible is not the same as correct. Always verify important information with an authoritative source.

**Very recent events:** The model has a knowledge cutoff, and while it can browse the web, web search is not always triggered and not always accurate. For breaking news or very recent developments, use a news source directly.

**Complex maths and calculations:** It can make arithmetic errors. For anything financial or mathematical, double-check the numbers.

**Australian-specific content:** ChatGPT was primarily trained on English-language internet content, which skews heavily American. It knows less about Australian laws, services, geography, and culture than you might expect. Always apply your own judgement about local relevance.

**Memory (free plan):** Each new conversation starts fresh. It does not remember who you are, what you discussed last time, or your preferences. The Plus plan enables memory, but even that has limits.

**Genuinely personal advice:** ChatGPT cannot see your medical records, knows nothing about your personal financial situation, and cannot give you legally or medically binding advice. Use it to understand, prepare, and learn — not to replace your GP or financial planner.

## Best Use Cases for Someone Aged 50+

1. **Preparing for a GP or specialist appointment.** "My doctor mentioned I might have mild osteoporosis. Can you explain what that means, what questions I should ask at my next appointment, and what the key treatment options are?" This produces a genuinely useful briefing. Take it with you.

2. **Writing tricky emails and letters.** Disputes with tradespeople, insurance complaints, requests to neighbours, letters to government departments — anything that requires careful wording. Paste in your rough draft and ask ChatGPT to polish it.

3. **Understanding documents.** Upload your energy retailer's terms and conditions, your superannuation statement, or a health fund comparison and ask what you actually need to know.

4. **Travel planning.** "Plan a two-week trip from Adelaide to Darwin, driving, in August, with a budget of $4,000 for accommodation. I enjoy history, wildlife, and small country towns." The itinerary it produces is a solid starting point.

5. **Creative and personal projects.** Family histories, memoirs, tributes at funerals, speeches at graduations, poems for cards — ChatGPT is a patient, tireless writing partner.

6. **Learning new things.** Want to understand blockchain? The Voice referendum debate? The difference between a fixed and variable mortgage rate? Ask. Keep asking follow-ups until it makes sense.

7. **Staying sharp.** Use it as a debate partner — ask it to argue the other side of a position you hold. Use it as a quiz-master. Use it to discuss books you have read.

## How to Get Started: Step by Step

1. Open your web browser (Chrome, Safari, Firefox — any will work) and go to **https://chat.openai.com**
2. Click "Sign up" in the top right corner
3. Enter your email address and create a password (or sign in with your existing Google or Apple account)
4. Verify your email address by clicking the link OpenAI sends you
5. You're in. You'll see a text box at the bottom of the screen. Click it and start typing.
6. Your first prompt could be something simple: **"Hello. Can you explain what you can help me with?"**
7. Read the response. Then follow up with a real question — something you've been curious about, or a practical task you need help with today.

**For voice mode:** On the mobile app (iPhone or Android), tap the headphones icon near the text box. This activates Advanced Voice Mode. Speak naturally; ChatGPT will listen and respond aloud.

## Five Specific Prompts for This Audience

**1. Before a medical appointment:**
> "I've been referred to a cardiologist for palpitations. I'm 63, otherwise healthy, and mildly anxious about the appointment. Can you give me: (a) a plain-English explanation of what a cardiologist looks for in this situation, (b) a list of 10 questions I should ask, and (c) any information I should bring with me?"

**2. Drafting a difficult email:**
> "I need to write a professional but firm email to my building's body corporate. They promised to fix the gate intercom three months ago and nothing has happened. I've emailed twice with no response. I want to be assertive but not aggressive, and I want to reference the relevant body corporate act. I live in Queensland. Can you draft this for me?"

**3. Understanding a document:**
> "I'm going to paste my health insurance extras policy. Can you summarise in plain English: (a) what is actually covered for dental, (b) what the waiting periods are, and (c) what catches people out that they don't realise until it's too late?" [Then paste the document text]

**4. Travel planning:**
> "I'd like to plan a trip from Melbourne to Hobart — fly in, take the ferry back on the Spirit of Tasmania. I want to spend two weeks, mainly exploring the east coast and national parks. I'm 68, reasonably fit, and my budget is around $5,000 excluding flights. I'd like a mix of accommodation — not just motels. Can you build me a day-by-day itinerary with estimated costs?"

**5. Learning something new:**
> "I keep hearing about 'the cloud' and I'm not entirely sure I understand it. Can you explain it to me as if I'm a reasonably intelligent 60-year-old who is comfortable with technology but never had anyone explain this simply? No jargon unless you immediately define it."

## Privacy and Data Considerations (Australian Law)

**What OpenAI does with your conversations:** By default, OpenAI uses conversations to improve its models. You can opt out of this in your settings: go to Settings > Data Controls and turn off "Improve the model for everyone."

**What to avoid typing:** Do not enter your full name, date of birth, Medicare number, TFN, bank account details, or passwords into ChatGPT. Treat it like a public conversation — useful, but not private.

**OpenAI's privacy policy:** OpenAI is a US company. Your data is stored on US servers and subject to US law. The Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles apply to Australian organisations using ChatGPT commercially, but as an individual user, you are primarily governed by OpenAI's terms of service.

**The OAIC position:** Australia's Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) has published guidance (2024-2025) noting that using AI tools with personal information triggers Privacy Act obligations for organisations. As an individual, exercise common sense: don't share sensitive personal information you would not share with a stranger.

**Memory feature:** If you enable memory on the Plus plan, ChatGPT retains information about you across sessions. You can review and delete what it has remembered at any time in Settings > Personalization > Memory.

## Honest Assessment: Is It Worth It?

The free plan is genuinely useful. For someone new to AI, starting with the free tier makes complete sense — it gives you access to a capable model and is sufficient for writing help, explanations, and planning tasks.

The Plus plan (AUD $35/month) is worth it if you use ChatGPT regularly for document analysis, image generation, or voice mode. If you use it three or four times a week for tasks that save you real time, the cost is easy to justify.

If you are unsure, use the free plan for a month first. Then decide.

The main reason to start with ChatGPT rather than another AI tool: it is the most widely known, which means there is more help available when you get stuck — more YouTube tutorials, more articles, more people in your life who have used it and can help.

**Verdict:** Excellent starting point for anyone new to AI. Free tier is a reasonable trial; Plus plan worth it for regular users.
