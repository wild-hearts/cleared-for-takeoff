## Where AI handles the writing-heavy parts

Most of a property week is words. Here is where AI takes the first draft off your plate so you can get back to the people who actually buy, sell and rent.

**Listing descriptions and property copy.** You feed it the facts (bedrooms, land size, the new kitchen, the school zone, the body corporate fee) and it drafts the description, the headline and the portal copy in seconds. You do not let it guess. If you did not give it the figure, it does not get to invent one.

**Owner and tenant communications.** The "we have three groups through, no offers yet" update to a vendor. The rent-arrears reminder that needs to be firm but not cold. The entry-notice email. AI drafts the awkward ones in plain, fair language and you adjust the tone before you hit send.

**Market updates and newsletters.** Paste in your own sales figures and suburb notes and it turns them into a readable monthly update. You verify every number against your source. AI is drafting the sentences, not auditing your data.

**Social posts and captions.** A just-listed post, an open-home reminder, a sold announcement, a short market comment. It gives you five options to pick from instead of you staring at a blank box on a Friday afternoon.

**Follow-up messages.** The "great to meet you at the open home" note, the "are you still looking?" check-in, the post-settlement thank you. The messages you know you should send and never quite get to.

**Long report into a plain summary.** A building and pest report, a strata search, a contract clause. AI turns the dense version into a short, clear summary you can talk a client through, while you keep the full document as the real record.

One rule sits over all of it. You verify every fact and figure before anything goes out. You stay compliant with your obligations and your state's rules, and you never let AI make a claim about a property that you have not checked yourself. It drafts the words fast. You are the one who confirms they are true, makes them sound like you, and signs off. That is the whole point: the writing gets quicker so the time goes back to the people.

## Common questions about AI in real estate

**Can it write my listings?**
It drafts them from the facts you give it. You hand it the details, it shapes the description and the copy, and then you verify every detail and add the local knowledge no model has: the street, the light at 4pm, the buyer it will suit. The draft is fast. The accuracy and the colour are yours.

**Is it compliant to use?**
You stay responsible for everything that goes out under your name. AI is a drafting tool, not a shortcut around your obligations. You verify the facts and figures, you make no misleading or auto-generated claims, and the human checks everything before it reaches a vendor, a tenant or a portal. Used that way, it is just a faster keyboard.

**Will it sound generic?**
Only if you let it. Generic copy comes from a lazy prompt and no edit. You steer it with real detail and your own brief, then edit the draft to your voice and to the specific property. Ten minutes of shaping turns a bland draft into something that sounds like you wrote it, because you finished it.
